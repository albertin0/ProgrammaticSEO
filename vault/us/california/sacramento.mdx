## What's the Real Deal for Your Run?

Look, the weather itself is comfortable, borderline pleasant for a late February day. You won't be sweating buckets or freezing your digits off. The big asterisk here is that AQI. An 89 isn't "stay inside or die," but it's enough to warrant caution, especially if you're prone to respiratory issues or planning a high-intensity workout. Think of it as running through a slight haze of whatever the city decided to kick up today.

On the ground, things are mostly clear. We just saw a flood warning for parts of the Sacramento River system up north (Tehama Bridge, Vina Woodson Bridge) expire this morning, so while the immediate danger has passed, remember that river levels can still be high. Always be smart near the American River Parkway Multi-Use Trail. Parts of Discovery Park, for example, have seen closures due to high water in the past, so keep an eye out for any lingering dampness or trail debris, especially after recent rainfall earlier this week that brought "nuisance flooding". Don't be that person who ignores a muddy, eroded section just to hit their mileage goal. Your ankles (and the trail maintenance crew) will thank you.
