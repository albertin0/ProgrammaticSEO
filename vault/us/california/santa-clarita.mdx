## The Rundown: Sun, Sweat, and Diesel Fumes

Today's a classic Santa Clarita February day: clear skies, and temperatures around 17.7°C (that's pushing 64°F for the Fahrenheit folks). Beautiful, right? Almost too beautiful. We just had some near-record breaking heat yesterday, and while it's cooled "a notch," we're still 10-15 degrees above normal for this time of year. Your body's still adjusting, so don't hit that hilly loop around the East Walker Ranch Open Space like it's a brisk fall morning.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Hydration isn't optional, it's foundational.** Even if it "feels nice," you're still working harder in warmer temps. And speaking of foundational, remember today is Repetitive Strain Injury Awareness Day. So, if you're thinking of suddenly cranking up your mileage or lifting heavy without proper form, remember your PT told you not to be stupid. Gradual progression, people.
</AlertBox>

## Breathing and Buzzing

Now, let's talk air. The Air Quality Index is sitting at 88. That's "moderate." The primary culprit? Nitrogen dioxide. So, while it's not "stay indoors and hermetically seal your house" bad, it's not pristine mountain air either. If you've got any kind of lung issues, or even if you just prefer not to inhale extra vehicle emissions, maybe dial back the intensity. Or at least avoid running directly alongside the I-5 during rush hour, which, let's be honest, you should be doing anyway.

**Mosquitoes are back, baby!** Thanks to the unseasonably warm weather after recent rains, those annoying bloodsuckers, including the invasive Aedes aegypti, are thriving. They're not just a nuisance; they carry diseases. Slap on that repellent, especially if you're hitting trails near standing water or anywhere shady like some of the lower sections of the paseos near Bouquet Creek.
</AlertBox>

## Construction Chaos

And finally, the eternal Santa Clarita lament: construction. The I-5 North County Enhancements Project is still chugging along through 2026. This means intermittent *overnight* closures for parts of The Old Road, Magic Mountain Parkway, and Rye Canyon Road, plus various I-5 ramps. So, if your morning commute to the Plum Canyon Park trailhead involves any of these, give yourself extra time, or find a new route.

More significantly, Bouquet Canyon Road is *closed* between Plum Canyon Road and David Way for about eleven months. They're rebuilding a bridge and expanding the flood control channel. This directly impacts travel and any trails connected to that stretch. While they promise "new trails" eventually, for now, it's a mess. If your usual route takes you through that area, you'll need to detour via Haskell Canyon Road or Copper Hill Drive.

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Bouquet Canyon Road is CLOSED.** Don't try to "just sneak through" or assume your usual trail access is unaffected if it's anywhere near Plum Canyon Road or David Way. It's closed for a reason, and that reason involves heavy machinery and deep holes. Safety first, people.
</AlertBox>

All in all, today's a mixed bag. The weather's pleasant but deceivingly warm, the air's okay but not perfect, and the city's still a construction zone. Get out there, but be smart about it.
