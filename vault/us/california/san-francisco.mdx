## Workout Hazards Report: February 26, 2026

You'd think a clear, cool day would be a no-brainer, but this is San Francisco. We specialize in making things complicated. While the weather gods are giving us a break, the city's infrastructure apparently isn't.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Air Quality Alert for Sensitive Groups:** The AQI is clocking in at 84 with nitrogen dioxide as the dominant pollutant. For most of us, it's a minor annoyance, but if you've got asthma or other respiratory issues, you might want to ease up on the intervals today. Seriously, don't pretend you're invincible. [cite: environmental_data]
</AlertBox>

Let's talk about actually *moving* around this city. The perennial construction on the I-80 and the Bay Bridge continues its reign of minor terror, with closures extending from last night into this morning. While these are mostly for drivers, if you're one of those ambitious souls who cycles the bridge or runs near the on/off ramps, be aware of increased traffic flow and potential detours around the Central and Bayshore Project areas.

More acutely for us ground-pounders, the SFMTA has graced us with a fresh batch of street closures **today until 10 a.m.** that will impact your morning routine, particularly around downtown. Expect the bike lane and one traffic lane on the **southbound Embarcadero** to be closed between North Point and Washington streets. Also, look out for full closures on Grant Avenue, Pacific Avenue, Stockton Street, and North Point Street in various sections. So much for that clear, uninterrupted waterfront jog, huh? You'll be dodging cones and grumpy tourists.

And if you thought a peaceful long run out west was in the cards, think again. The trail along **Skyline Boulevard at Lake Merced** is still shut down through late June for reconstruction. You'll have to take the marked pedestrian detour through Harding Golf Course. Just try not to get clocked by a rogue golf ball while you're at it.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Tip for Runners & Cyclists:** Always check current street closures before heading out, especially if your route takes you through downtown or near major construction zones. The city moves at its own chaotic pace. A quick glance at the SFMTA advisories can save you a world of pain and wasted mileage.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Ongoing Construction Headaches:** The San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge Joint Rehabilitation Project means overnight and early morning ramp closures are a regular feature. While this specific one wraps up by 7 AM today, it's a constant reminder that our city's arteries are always being poked and prodded. Plan alternate routes if you rely on these areas for your commute or workout.
</AlertBox>

So, yeah, it's clear and cool. Go outside. Just remember you're in San Francisco, and a little urban grit is part of the package. Don't be surprised if your "clear" run turns into an impromptu obstacle course.
