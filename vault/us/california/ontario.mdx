## Breaking Down Today's Hazards

### High-Elevation Trails Are a No-Go!

<AlertBox type="danger">
#### Mount Baldy Trails are CLOSED until end of day today, Feb 26th!
Listen up: the U.S. Forest Service has extended closures for multiple trails in the Mount Baldy area, within the San Gabriel Mountains National Monument, through today, February 26, 2026. This isn't for fun; it's because of hazardous winter conditions. We're talking snow, ice, high winds, and visibility so bad you could walk right off a cliff. Trails like the *Ontario Peak Trail* are specifically listed. Seriously, stay off them. You think your phone will get a signal for help up there when things go sideways? Think again. Your lungs will be fine, but your joints (and bones) might not be after a slip and fall.
</AlertBox>

### AQI for the Sensitive Set

<AlertBox type="warning">
#### Moderate Air Quality – Keep an Eye on Your Lungs
With an AQI of 53 and inhalable particulate matter dominating, today's air quality is teetering on "unhealthy for sensitive groups." If you've got asthma, COPD, or just generally find yourself coughing after a hard workout, take it easy. Maybe skip that long run and opt for an indoor session, or at least keep your outdoor activity short and low-intensity. It's not the end of the world, but it's not the freshest air you'll ever breathe either.
</AlertBox>

### Construction Zone Ahead

<AlertBox type="warning">
#### Sultana Avenue Storm Drain Project – Watch Your Step
Construction continues for the Sultana Avenue Storm Drain Project. Expect partial street closures and temporary traffic control. If your usual route takes you through areas like Columbia Avenue, especially near Fifth Street and Euclid Avenue, you'll need to be extra vigilant. Don't assume drivers see you, and keep an eye out for uneven surfaces or unexpected detours. Your ankles will thank you.
</AlertBox>

### So, Should You Go Out?

Look, the temperature is perfect – a gorgeous 23°C (73.4°F) and clear skies. Low pollen is a bonus for most of us. But if your workout involves heading for the hills around Mount Baldy, absolutely do not. The closures are legitimate. For everyone else, keep your run or ride away from the construction zones, and if you're sensitive to air quality, maybe stick to a walk or an indoor workout. Your body will thank you for not pushing it through questionable air.
