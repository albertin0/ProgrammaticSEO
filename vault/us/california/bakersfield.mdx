## The Rundown for February 26, 2026

Today's shaping up to be one of those days where the weather itself isn't trying to kill you, which, in Bakersfield, is a win in my book. We're looking at clear skies and a lovely 17.6°C (63.7°F). It's basically perfect running weather. Don't forget that just this morning, the San Joaquin Valley had chances for dense fog, so if you were out early, hopefully, you were staying extra vigilant. For now, visibility is good.

The good news for your lungs is that pollen is sitting at a low, practically "none" level. Breathe deep, allergy sufferers, but maybe not *too* deep, because our air quality is still hovering in the "Moderate" range, specifically for inhalable particulate matter (PM2.5/PM10). This means if you've got asthma or any other respiratory issues, you might want to scale back on any prolonged, heavy outdoor exertion. It's not "stay indoors and hermetically seal yourself" bad, but it's not "fill your lungs with pure mountain air" good either.

<AlertBox type="warning">
  **Air Quality Advisory:** The AQI is currently 55-59, categorized as Moderate. The dominant pollutants are particulate matters (&lt;10µm and PM2.5). Individuals with respiratory sensitivities should consider reducing prolonged or heavy exertion outdoors.
</AlertBox>

Now, for the runners and cyclists among us, you need to be aware of some major inconveniences. A significant portion of the **Kern River Multi-Use Trail** is closed. Specifically, a section on the Stockdale Ranch extension, about a mile north of the main bike path, is shut down from February 23rd through April 19th for retention basin construction. So, if your usual route takes you there, plan an alternate. You'll likely encounter other detours around town too, with Eye Street closed for sewer work and various other road projects underway this fiscal year. Keep an eye on local city websites for specific construction updates.
