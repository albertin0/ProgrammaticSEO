## The Rundown: Don't Be a Dummy Out There

You know I'm all about getting those miles in, but not at the expense of your actual health. Today's one of those days where a little common sense goes a long way.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Air Quality & Your Lungs
With the AQI sitting at 58 and particulate matter being the main culprit, sensitive individuals – that's anyone with asthma, bronchitis, or just generally grumpy lungs – should seriously consider reducing prolonged or heavy outdoor exertion. Even if you're a seasoned runner, an hour-long tempo run today might leave you feeling a bit more winded than usual, and it's not because you're out of shape. That PM10 can increase the severity of asthma attacks and reduce your body's ability to fight infections. If you *must* run outside, consider sticking to lower intensity and shorter durations.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Construction Zones Are Still a Thing
The city is always building something, and that means dust and detours. The SR-22 (Garden Grove Freeway) Improvement Project is ongoing, with various ramp and street closures that might affect your route if you're anywhere near it. More locally, there's been extensive construction and demolition work impacting the intersection of Garden Grove Boulevard and Harbor Boulevard, which will complicate traffic for a while. Plan your routes to avoid these high-traffic, high-dust areas. And for those of you trying to get across town, remember there are lane reductions on eastbound Westminster Avenue between Newhope Street and Harbor Boulevard for road work until tomorrow. Stay alert, runners.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
### Mind the Mobs at Garden Grove Park
If you're heading to Garden Grove Park for your workout, be aware of the community event this afternoon. While it's great for the community, it means more people, maybe some kids darting around, and potentially less space for you to really get into your rhythm. Maybe try a less popular route or time your run for earlier in the morning.
