<AlertBox type="danger">
#### Santa Ana River Trail is a Minefield of Closures
If your route involves the Santa Ana River Trail, be prepared for significant disruptions. Major detours and outright closures are in effect, especially between Riverside Avenue/Main Street and Columbia Avenue, and near the Market Street Bridge to CA 60. This isn't a minor inconvenience; these are long-term projects expected to continue through July 2026. OC Public Works is also doing "remedial repairs" between Weir Canyon Road/La Palma Avenue and Lincoln Avenue through *this very month*, which means you might encounter flag personnel and have to dismount your bike through active work zones. Don't assume your usual stretch is open. Check local maps or be ready to reroute on the fly.
</AlertBox>

Seriously, it's not worth risking a collision with heavy machinery or getting lost down some sketchy detour just to hit your mileage. Find an alternative or prepare for a broken rhythm. While a new section of the Santa Ana River Trail did open in San Bernardino recently, that doesn't help you much if you're sticking to the Santa Ana proper.

Beyond the SART, the city's general infrastructure overhaul continues. Santa Ana is pouring millions into various projects, including the reconstruction of Memorial Park's Aquatic Center and improvements at Centennial Park, both slated to wrap up by the end of 2026. While these will be great when they're done, for now, they mean more construction zones and potential local road impacts.

<AlertBox type="info">

Given the extensive trail closures, today might be better for a structured workout in a known park or a neighborhood run. Santiago Park might offer a pleasant alternative, though even there, improvements and construction around other city parks mean keeping an eye out for local disruptions.
