### On Your Marks, Get Set... But Check the Detours

So, you're looking at a pretty sweet day for an outdoor workout in Santa Ana. The sky's clear, the air quality is good, and you won't be melting into the pavement. That's a solid win for your lungs and joints, especially if you're prone to seasonal sniffles or heatstroke from our typical summer inferno.

However, if your usual route involves anything near the **Santa Ana River Trail**, hold your horses. It's not all sunshine and smooth sailing out there. OC Public Works has been busy with "remedial repairs" on sections of the trail between Weir Canyon Road/La Palma Avenue and Lincoln Avenue since January, and that work is expected to continue through February. Meaning, right now. You might encounter flag personnel, shifting work areas, and find yourself needing to dismount and walk your bike through certain sections.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Santa Ana River Trail Alert:** Major detours and closures are in effect on the Santa Ana River Trail, particularly between Riverside Ave./Main St. and Columbia Ave., and near the Market St. Bridge to CA 60, through July 2026. If you're planning a long ride or run, be prepared for significant rerouting and potential dismounts.
</AlertBox>

Beyond the river trail, the city's also in a constant state of growth. Santa Ana saw a whopping $772 million in building permits in 2023, and there are big projects like the Memorial Park Aquatic Center reconstruction and improvements at Centennial Park aiming for a 2026 finish. While these are great for the city long-term, they can mean local disruptions, noise, and unexpected detours on neighborhood streets. Keep your head on a swivel.
