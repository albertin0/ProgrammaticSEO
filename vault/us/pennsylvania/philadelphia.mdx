### Why today is NOT your PR day:

<BulletList>
  <li>**Ice, Ice, Baby:** We just got slammed with a blizzard that dumped over a foot of snow on the city between February 22-24. While the skies are clear now, with temperatures hovering around freezing (-0.9°C), expect treacherous patches of black ice and lingering snow drifts on sidewalks, residential streets, and especially any unplowed sections of trails or bike lanes. Even major routes that were plowed still have slick spots.</li>
  <li>**Watch Your Head!** The recent storm brought some seriously high winds (up to 45 mph) which caused significant tree damage across the city. Parks and Recreation crews have been responding to dozens of downed trees, and they're still out there inspecting, because heavy snow and winds can compromise even more trees into the spring. Keep an eye out for hanging branches, especially if you're venturing off the main drag in Fairmount Park.</li>
  <li>**AQI Ain't Great, Just "Okay":** Air Quality Index is moderate at 56, with fine particulate matter as the dominant pollutant. While it's not a "Code Orange" day, if you're sensitive, you might feel it on a hard effort. Pair that with the cold, dry air, and your lungs might not thank you for an intense session.</li>

## Trail Talk: Schuylkill River Trail

The beloved Schuylkill River Trail, especially the sections running right through the city, is going to be a mixed bag. The main paved portions likely saw some plowing, but don't assume clear sailing. Those riverside stretches, particularly on the lower end towards Paine's Park and the Water Works, are prime real estate for lingering ice in shaded areas. You'll want to be extra cautious.

<AlertBox type="warning">
  **Caution: Schuylkill River Trail Sections Beyond City Limits**
  If you're heading upriver into Montgomery County, be aware of ongoing construction. AT&T is doing work near the SRT in Norristown and Conshohocken this month, so expect some activity and potential minor disruptions. Further out in Pottstown, the Keim Street Bridge construction is expected to impact trail users along Industrial Highway through 2026 with detours and intermittent closures. Always check local updates before you plan a long haul.
</AlertBox>

### General Wisdom for Today

<AlertBox type="info">
  **Dress Warm and Layer Up:** It's cold out there. Layers are your friend. Think wicking base layers, an insulating mid-layer, and a windproof/water-resistant outer shell. Don't forget gloves, a hat, and good socks.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="danger">
  **Footing is Everything:** Whether you're running, walking, or cycling, assume there's ice. Choose footwear with good grip, and if you're running, consider adding traction devices like Yaktrax. Take smaller, shuffling steps on questionable surfaces. A fall on ice is a surefire way to end up with a sprain or worse.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
  **Plan Your Route (and Your Bailout):** Stick to well-trafficked, likely-plowed areas if you can. If you're exploring, have a backup plan for when you hit an unpassable icy patch. And for the love of all that is holy, tell someone where you're going.
</AlertBox>

Look, the sun's out, which is a nice change, but don't let the clear skies fool you into thinking it's prime running weather. This is a day for smart choices and a healthy dose of Philly grit.
