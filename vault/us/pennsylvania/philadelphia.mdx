## Today's Rundown

Alright, listen up, runners and walkers. You're probably itching to get out after being snowed in, and I get it. The sun's out, the sky's blue, and the mercury is actually above freezing at 7.8°C (that's about 46°F for those still stuck in Fahrenheit-land). But here's the brutally honest truth: that recent nor'easter dumped a *ton* of snow on us, with some areas seeing well over a foot. Even though the city lifted the official snow emergency yesterday, that doesn't magically clear every path, curb cut, or shadowy corner.

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Watch Your Step! Black Ice is Your New Arch-Nemesis.**
That warmer temperature? It's a double-edged sword. It's melting the snow during the day, creating puddles that will absolutely refreeze into invisible sheets of death overnight or in shaded areas. Seriously, a sprained ankle or worse on the Schuylkill River Trail is not how you want to kick off your Friday. Stick to well-trafficked, plowed routes, and assume every dark patch of pavement is a hidden ice trap. The city crews have been working hard, but they can't get everywhere instantly.
</AlertBox>

The air quality, sitting at an AQI of 63, is in the "Moderate" category. Fine particulate matter is the dominant pollutant, which isn't exactly a fresh mountain breeze. While it's not a Code Orange like we've seen recently, if you have any respiratory issues, take it easy. We just got that new air monitoring network online, so at least we're getting more real-time data now, which is something.

Pollen is low, so at least your sinuses get a break today. Small victories, right?

<AlertBox type="info">
**Head on a Swivel.**
With trash and recycling collections still delayed from the storm, some sidewalks might have extra obstacles. Plus, it's Philly; there's always some unexpected bit of construction or a rogue pothole lurking. Keep your eyes peeled and your wits about you.
</AlertBox>

Overall, if you're going out, choose wisely. The Schuylkill River Trail might look inviting along the open stretches, but those underpasses and tree-lined sections are likely still treacherous. Maybe stick to the sidewalks of Broad Street or a well-cleared park loop if you absolutely need to move. Your body will thank you for not risking a slip-and-slide adventure.
