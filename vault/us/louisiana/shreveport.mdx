## Construction Continues to Be a Way of Life

If you're planning a long ride or run near the I-20 corridor, particularly around the areas connecting Shreveport and Bossier City, remember that massive I-20 Major Rehabilitation Project is still kicking. While they claim it's in the "final phase" and several interchanges are open, expect the usual intermittent lane closures and general chaos, especially if you're trying to navigate nearby streets. Commercial vehicles are being detoured onto I-220, which means less truck traffic on I-20, but potentially more on that alternate route if you're out that way.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Air Quality Alert for Sensitive Groups:** While the overall AQI is moderate at 69, with fine particulate matter (&lt;2.5µm) as the dominant pollutant, individuals with respiratory issues like asthma or COPD should consider adjusting their outdoor exertion. Monitor symptoms and scale back if needed. Don't be a hero; your lungs will thank you.
</AlertBox>

Seriously, those fine particulates might just feel like a bit of a dry throat for the average runner, but for anyone prone to lung funk, it’s worth being mindful. We also still have burn bans in effect across much of the region, which means wildfire smoke could always sneak in and mess with our air quality, even if it's not a primary concern today.

And speaking of traffic, recent DOTD reports from just a couple of days ago listed several lane and ramp closures in Caddo Parish, including parts of I-20 westbound at Fairfield Ave and on LA 3132. These aren't trail-specific, but if you're running or cycling along **Clyde Fant Parkway**, be aware that nearby road work or associated traffic snarls could make access or crossing points a little more annoying than usual. Always keep your head on a swivel when you're near major roads, because Shreveport drivers aren't known for their patience.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Heads Up on Road Conditions:** Beyond the major I-20 project, keep an eye out for more localized construction. The city has a whole "Capital Projects" initiative underway, improving everything from streets to parks, so you never know when you might hit an unexpected detour or freshly dug-up section of sidewalk.
