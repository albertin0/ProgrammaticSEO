Don't let that pleasant breeze fool you; while the air feels good, there are still a few New Orleans-specific annoyances that can throw a wrench in your outdoor plans today.

<LungsJointsScore score={7} />

<BulletList>
*   **Respiratory Alert:** While the pollen count is low, the Air Quality Index is sitting at a "Moderate" 67, with inhalable particulate matter (&lt;10µm) being the dominant pollutant. This isn't "stay inside" bad, but if you're sensitive, you might feel it. Give those lungs a little extra thought.
*   **Construction Chaos:** Forget clear sailing, especially in New Orleans East. The eastbound lanes of Morrison Road, between Mayo Boulevard and Bundy Road, are shut down for a major improvement project that’s expected to last through July 2027. That means sidewalks, too, are impacted, forcing you into detours or worse, closer to traffic. Even if you're not out East, expect sporadic lane closures on main arteries like US 90/Claiborne Ave. in Orleans Parish.
*   **Mardi Gras Mess (Lingering):** While Fat Tuesday has passed, the French Quarter is still dealing with construction dragging into "festival season," making those narrow sidewalks even more of a pain to navigate. Don't expect a pristine running surface if you're trying to get some miles in around the historic districts.
</BulletList>

Today's 23.7°C (around 74.7°F) and clear skies are, objectively, fantastic for a run or a walk. But this isn't some generic suburban trail, this is New Orleans. And that means you've always got to contend with our unique brand of urban grit.

That moderate AQI, with its PM10, is why I'm docking the "Lungs" score a bit. It's not a disaster, but for anyone with asthma or other respiratory issues, it's enough to consider a slightly shorter route or sticking to areas with less vehicle exhaust. Think City Park trails rather than dodging traffic on the shoulder of a major road.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Watch Your Step on Morrison Road:** If your route takes you anywhere near Morrison Road in New Orleans East, be prepared for significant disruptions. Sidewalks are affected by this long-term project, pushing pedestrians and cyclists into potentially unsafe areas. Plan an alternate route or be extra vigilant. This isn't just an inconvenience; it's a legitimate hazard to your ankles and your safety.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
**Hydration is Still Key:** Even with pleasant temperatures, New Orleans humidity can still sneak up on you. Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. Don't be that person I see cramping up halfway through a 10K.
</AlertBox>

So, yeah, get out there if you want to. The weather's mostly playing nice. Just don't expect a smooth ride, especially where the city's perpetually "improving" our infrastructure. Your knees will thank you for picking your path wisely.
