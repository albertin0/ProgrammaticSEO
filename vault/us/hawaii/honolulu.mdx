## Today's Honolulu Workout Outlook: Breezy with a Side of Caution

Alright, Honolulu, if you're lacing up today, Thursday, February 26, 2026, the weather looks clear enough at 22°C (about 72°F). But don't let that fool you into thinking it's all rainbows and plumerias out there. We've got a few things to keep you from becoming a statistic.

First off, let's talk about the air. Our Air Quality Index (AQI) is sitting at a "moderate" 66, with ozone as the dominant pollutant. While it's not red-alert bad, it's not exactly pristine mountain air either. If you've got any kind of respiratory sensitivity, you might feel this one a bit more. Low pollen is a small win, though.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Watch for Gusty Winds

Expect breezy conditions with trade winds hitting around 15 mph and gusts up to 20 mph, especially along ridges and exposed coasts. This might not sound like much, but it can make running feel like a constant uphill battle and adds a layer of instability for outdoor yoga or balancing exercises. Secure those hats, people.
</AlertBox>

Now, for those of you thinking about a dip in the ocean or a shoreline run, hold your horses. The National Weather Service still has a High Surf Advisory in effect for east-facing shores through 6 p.m. HST today. We're talking larger breaking waves and strong currents out there. Yesterday, a swimmer had to be rescued from the waters near **Halona Blowhole**. That should tell you everything you need to know. Don't be that person. Seriously.

<AlertBox type="danger">
### High Surf & Strong Currents: Stay Out!

The High Surf Advisory for east-facing shores means strong breaking waves and dangerous currents are present. Lifeguards *will* close beaches if conditions warrant it, and you should respect those warnings. Swimming and ocean activities are genuinely hazardous today.
</AlertBox>

If you're hitting the trails, most state parks and forest areas, including Nā Ala Hele hiking trails, generally reopened after earlier severe weather. However, always double-check specific trails. The Nā Ala Hele website still lists several Oahu trails, like the Tantalus-Arboretum Trail, as "Closed Inactive". Don't just assume your favorite spot is clear; call ahead or check official websites. Muddy, slippery conditions could still be present in rain-affected areas, even if recent flooding alerts have expired.

And for our road runners and cyclists, prepare for detours and delays. The Hawaii Department of Transportation is knee-deep in roadwork. Expect various lane closures on major arteries like the H-1 Freeway, Likelike Highway, and Kamehameha Highway for inspections and ongoing projects, including the H-1 Salt Lake Rehabilitation Project that runs through February. A pedestrian was involved in a critical motor vehicle collision just a couple of days ago near Alexander and Dole Street, so if you're sharing the road, make yourself visible and be hyper-aware.

### Stay Hydrated & Visible
