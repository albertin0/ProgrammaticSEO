Look, I get it. The temp is a mild 7.9°C (around 46°F) and the sky is clear, which usually screams "get outside." But don't let that fool you into thinking it's an easy day. That 30 mph wind gust isn't just a nuisance; it can seriously mess with your balance, particularly if you're hitting some of the rockier sections of trails like those over at John Bryan State Park where "mud with gaiters and/or change of shoes" is already recommended for weekend hikes. Combine that with the "fair" hiking conditions and "slippery surfaces" warning, and you've got a recipe for a twisted ankle.

Then there's the air. A moderate AQI of 73 with fine particulate matter means your respiratory system is working a little harder than usual. If you've got any underlying lung issues, or even if you just prefer to not feel like you're sucking down car exhaust, today might not be your day for an intense tempo run on the Great Miami River Trail, especially near the I-75 reconstruction zone.

Speaking of I-75, the area between SR 4 and Needmore Road is a perpetual mess with reconstruction, traffic shifts, and ramp closures, like the Wagner-Ford Road ramp to I-75 South, which is closed through Fall 2026. That kind of disruption spills over to local streets and can make navigating surrounding paths and sidewalks a nightmare, not to mention the increased vehicle emissions right where you're trying to breathe. Other projects, like the Salem Avenue Reconstruction and Keowee Street Safety Improvements, are also kicking off or ongoing, meaning less predictable routes throughout the city.
