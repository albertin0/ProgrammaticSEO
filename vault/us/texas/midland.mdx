Don't let the calendar fool you into thinking it's still "nice" out there. Today, February 26th, is shaping up to be a deceptively tough day for outdoor workouts in Midland. We're looking at a blazing 24.7°C (that's 76-77°F for the Fahrenheit die-hards) under clear skies, but don't get too comfortable. This isn't your grandma's mild winter day; it's a dry, windy oven waiting to happen.

The National Weather Service has issued a Red Flag Warning for the Permian Basin, including our neck of the woods, from late this morning into the early evening. This means critical fire weather conditions are present: low humidity, increased westerly winds, and fuels that are drier than a West Texas comedian's jokes. Seriously, think twice before doing anything that could spark a blaze, whether it's flicking a cigarette butt (don't you dare) or dragging a chain. We don't need to add a smoky haze to our run.

And speaking of haze, our Air Quality Index is currently sitting at a "moderate" 70, with ozone being the dominant pollutant. If you're susceptible to respiratory issues, that's enough to make your lungs feel like they've run a marathon before you even tie your shoes. Combine that with the heat, and you're just asking for trouble if you push too hard.
