That's enough to feel like you're running uphill both ways and can throw off your balance, especially near the open Bayfront or along the Cole Park trails. Don't be that person who gets blown into traffic.
*   **Construction Zone Chaos:** The city council just approved a hefty $7.1 million contract for upgrades at Greenwood Sports Complex yesterday, and a $6.5 million grant for North Beach renourishment also got the green light. While those aren't impacting your run *today*, know that our city is a perpetual construction site. More immediately, expect daily single-lane closures along **South Staples Street**, especially between Williams Drive and Saratoga Boulevard. If Staples is part of your usual route, plan detours unless you enjoy dodging cones and impatient drivers.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Wind Advisory:** Those southerly winds are no joke. Running or cycling against a 25 mph headwind is a brutal workout, but a crosswind can be dangerous, particularly on exposed bridges or along the Shoreline Boulevard. If you're on a bike, seriously reconsider your route or stick to more sheltered areas. Even walking can be a struggle, and sand from the beaches might be whipping around.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Construction Navigation:** South Staples Street is a hot mess with ongoing daily lane closures. Don't assume drivers see you; they're probably already annoyed by the traffic. If you're running, stick to sidewalks where available, and if cycling, find an alternate route that minimizes interaction with these construction zones. A sudden swerve from a vehicle avoiding a cone is not how you want to end your run.
</AlertBox>

**Hydration & Sun Protection:** It's a clear, warm day. Even with the wind, you'll sweat. Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. Slather on that sunscreen – the sun doesn't care about the wind chill factor.
</AlertBox>
