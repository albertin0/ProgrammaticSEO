### Air Quality: Breathe Easy (or try to)

Let's talk about the air. Your typical air quality index (AQI) might be sitting at "Moderate" today at 58, but don't get complacent. The real kicker is the "inhalable particulate matter" floating around, apparently from residual smoke making its way up from central-southern Mexico and the Yucatan Peninsula. The city is specifically warning that PM levels are in the "Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups" range. If you've got asthma, COPD, or just don't fancy sucking down tiny bits of international campfire, maybe rethink that long run. Even for us iron-lunged types, it means less efficient oxygen exchange and more irritation. Low pollen is a small mercy, I guess, but it barely moves the needle when your lungs are fighting invisible smoke.

### Road and Trail Conditions: Dodge the Cones

While some major projects, like Alameda Street from Louisiana Avenue to Texan Trail, are thankfully *completed* as of Monday, don't expect smooth sailing everywhere. Construction on Yorktown Boulevard, particularly between Rodd Field Road and the Oso Creek Mud Bridge, still has westbound lanes closed, forcing two-way traffic onto the eastbound side. If you usually run or bike that stretch leading to the Oso Bay Wetlands Preserve, be ready for narrower paths, confused drivers, and detours. Same goes for Commodores Drive near Aquarius Street; inside lanes are shut down until late March. Stay alert out there; a distracted driver plus a congested lane is a bad combo for a runner.
