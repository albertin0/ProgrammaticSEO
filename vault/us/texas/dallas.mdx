Alright, Dallas, let's talk turkey. We've got clear skies and a pleasant 18.6°C working for us today, making that run around **White Rock Lake** or even a quick loop on the Katy Trail feel pretty sweet from a pure temperature standpoint. Enjoy that before the "significant cool-down" hits this weekend.

But don't let the sunshine fool you. Your lungs are going to be doing some heavy lifting, and not the good kind. With a "High" pollen count, every breath is a gamble. Seriously, preemptive antihistamines are your friend, or you'll be snot-rocketing your way through mile two.

Then there's the air quality. An AQI of 65 is squarely in "Moderate" territory, thanks largely to ozone. Moderate means it's generally okay for most folks, but if you've got any underlying respiratory issues, like asthma or just weak lungs from too many late nights at Lee Harvey's, you're going to feel it. That tightness isn't just your form; it's ozone. The Texas Commission on Environmental Quality forecast for Dallas-Fort Worth even mentioned PM2.5 for today, so you're getting a double-whammy of airborne crap.

### Mind the Motors (Especially Tonight)
If your workout involves getting to a trail or running near major roadways, be aware of ongoing construction. Northbound US-75 (Central Expressway) between Northwest Highway and I-635 has three left lanes closed nightly until 5 a.m. today. Likewise, Northeast Loop 820 (Tarrant County, but still North Texas) has full closures overnight. This means more idling cars, more exhaust, and generally more grit in the air. For those trying to get a run in downtown, remember the I-30 Canyon Project still has long-term street closures and active work zones where pedestrian access is a no-go.
</AlertBox>

Regionally, there's also an "elevated fire threat" west of Highway 281 today, so keep an eye out for any haze or burning smells, even if you're far from the fire-prone areas. While our immediate Dallas AQI doesn't reflect heavy smoke, things can change quickly with the wind.

My advice? If you're susceptible to allergies or have any lung issues, keep your runs shorter and maybe dial back the intensity. Or, just suck it up and get on a treadmill. I know, I know, sacrilege, but sometimes indoor air is just... cleaner. Or at least less filled with tree sperm and ozone.

If you're really struggling with the pollen and moderate AQI, consider a saline nasal rinse *before* and *after* your run. It won't fix everything, but it can help clear out some of the irritants from your nasal passages, making breathing a little less miserable. Your sinuses will thank you.
