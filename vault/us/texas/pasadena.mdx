The low pollen count is another win. You know the drill, one wrong step and you're sneezing your way through what was supposed to be a peaceful run. Today, your face should remain snot-free, at least from nature's attacks.

Now, for the fly in the ointment: the Air Quality Index. At 60, it's "Moderate." The dominant pollutant is fine particulate matter (&lt;2.5µm). While 60 isn't terrible, and most healthy individuals won't notice much, if you've got asthma, COPD, or are just generally sensitive to lung irritants, you might feel it. AccuWeather even called it "Poor" for sensitive groups in their forecast, though our current Ambee data says 60. My physical therapy brain tells you to listen to your body, not just the number. If you feel any irritation or shortness of breath, back off. There's no medal for pushing through an AQI warning.

*   **Hydrate:** Always. Especially when the air isn't perfectly clean.

*   **Listen to Your Body:** This isn't just PT dogma; it's common sense. If your throat feels scratchy or you're coughing more than usual, head inside.
