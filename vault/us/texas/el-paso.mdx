The morning temperature of 16.6°C (that's about 62°F for those still in denial about Celsius) is pretty much ideal for kicking off a run or a hike up at Franklin Mountains State Park. But don't let that fool you. The forecast suggests we'll be pushing into the high 20s Celsius (mid-80s Fahrenheit) by the afternoon. <AlertBox type="warning">
### Watch Your Step, El Paso!
Remember those high winds that tore through here about a week and a half ago? While city crews have been doing their thing, there's still a good chance you'll find lingering debris, fallen branches, or even some rogue trash on sidewalks, bike paths, and unpaved trails. Keep your eyes peeled, especially if you're pushing the pace, to avoid a face-plant or a twisted ankle. Nobody wants that kind of souvenir.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="danger">
### Fire Restrictions Still a Thing
Just because the immediate winds aren't screaming doesn't mean the wildfire risk has vanished. El Paso County was under Stage II Fire Restrictions as of earlier this week due to dry conditions and previous high winds. While there aren't any active fires reported in our immediate vicinity today, the general dryness and regional risk are real. Don't be "that guy" who starts a brush fire. No open flames, no flicking cigarette butts (ever, seriously), and if you're running deep into the desert, be extra cautious.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
### Hydration Isn't Just a Suggestion
With those temperatures climbing throughout the day, your water bottle is your best friend. Carry more than you think you'll need, especially if you're hitting longer distances or tackling elevations. Start hydrating *before* you leave the house, and don't wait until you feel thirsty. If you're out during peak afternoon heat, consider scaling back your intensity or postponing until the evening. Your body will thank you.
</AlertBox>

Overall, a solid green light for outdoor activity, just apply some common sense about the sun, the heat, and where you're putting your feet.
