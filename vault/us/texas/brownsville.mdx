## The Nitty Gritty

### Weather & Heat

It’s sunny, it’s hot, and the air is thick enough to chew on. Your body's going to be working overtime just to keep cool. The Brownsville Fire Department has already been busy responding to heat exhaustion calls. Don't be their next statistic.

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Extreme Heat Hazard!** With temperatures feeling like 32°C (90°F) and clear skies, the risk of heat exhaustion and heat stroke is high. If you're out running, cycling, or doing anything more strenuous than walking to your fridge, plan for early mornings or late evenings. Hydrate *before*, *during*, and *after* your workout, and wear light, moisture-wicking clothes. Don't be a hero; know the signs of overheating and get inside if you feel them.
</AlertBox>
