## The Daily Grind in Arlington: February 26, 2026

Alright, Arlington. We've got clear skies and a comfortable 18.6°C (65.5°F) out there today, which, on paper, sounds like prime running weather. Don't fall for it completely. The devil, as always, is in the details – specifically, the air you're breathing and the ground you're covering.

### Air Quality & Allergy Report

Let's be blunt: your respiratory system is getting a workout whether you want it to or not. The pollen levels are currently high, which is just fantastic news for anyone with seasonal allergies. If you’re prone to sneezing fits or watery eyes, you’ve been warned.

To add insult to injury, our Air Quality Index (AQI) is sitting at a moderate 78, with ozone as the dominant pollutant. This isn't "stay indoors and seal the windows" bad, but it’s definitely in the yellow-light zone, especially for anyone with pre-existing lung conditions. So, if you’re asthmatic or just generally sensitive to bad air, consider dialing back that tempo run or sticking to indoor facilities like Texas Health Fitness Center Arlington Memorial.

### Trail Talk & Hazards

Now, for those of us who prefer actual pavement or dirt underfoot to a treadmill:
There's an "elevated fire threat" today along and west of Highway 281. While that’s not directly *in* the heart of Arlington, if your usual long run takes you toward the western edges of the metroplex, be extra cautious. A small spark can turn into a big problem quickly when conditions are dry. Stick to areas where you know there's less dry brush, and definitely don't be flicking anything.

<AlertBox type="danger">
  **Fire Threat West of Highway 281:** An elevated fire threat is present. Avoid any activities that could potentially spark a fire, especially in dry, grassy areas. Your run isn't worth a wildfire.
</AlertBox>

Closer to home, if you're a fan of the downtown routes, you need to know a section of the **Center St Trail** between S Center St and Hosack St is currently closed. This closure has been in effect since early January and is slated to continue through early March, so don't count on that segment being open for your run today. You’ll need to reroute.

And if River Legacy Park is your jam, just a heads-up: while some major trail improvements were completed last year, and a connection to Fort Worth was anticipated for January 2026, there’s still ongoing work and potential reroutes due to erosion. Don't be surprised if you encounter some detours or less-than-perfect surfaces, especially if you're venturing towards the Trinity River portions. Always stay aware of your surroundings and any posted signage.

<AlertBox type="info">
  **Trail Construction & Reroutes:** The Center St Trail is closed between S Center St and Hosack St until early March. Plan alternate routes. At River Legacy Park, ongoing construction and erosion mitigation mean you might still encounter localized issues.
</AlertBox>

So, enjoy the sunshine, but keep your head up, your allergies in check, and know your routes.
