### The Real Talk

Look, it's Memphis in late February. We're usually either freezing our butts off or dodging a sudden downpour that turns every pothole into a wading pool. Today, we've got decent temps and no immediate rain, which is a win for your motivation to get outside. Your knees and hips will thank you for the mild weather.

However, let's not pretend that "moderate" air quality with fine particulate matter is a non-issue. I've seen too many runners push through "just a little bit of haze" and end up wheezing for the rest of the day. A 78 AQI, especially with PM2.5, means sensitive groups might feel it, and honestly, if you're doing anything more strenuous than a stroll, your lungs are working harder. Some reports are even calling today's air quality "poor" and "unhealthy for sensitive groups," advising to limit outdoor activity. Don't be a hero.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Air Quality Advisory for Sensitive Lungs
That AQI of 78, with fine particulate matter as the main culprit, is walking a fine line. If you're asthmatic, have COPD, or just feel easily winded, seriously consider taking your run indoors today. Your lungs are trying to filter out tiny crap, and that's extra work you don't need during a hard workout. Keep an eye on local air quality updates, but my advice: err on the side of caution.
</AlertBox>

And for the love of all that is holy, stay off the Wolf River Trails. I've said it once, I'll say it a thousand times: when a trail is closed, it's closed for a reason. Flooding earlier this month did a number on them, and Shelby Farms staff are still assessing the damage and clearing debris. Trying to navigate a compromised trail system isn't "challenging yourself"; it's asking for a twisted ankle, or worse. There's plenty of other concrete around if you need to get your miles in. Try the Shelby Farms Greenline or just stick to your neighborhood streets.

### Wolf River Trails Closed!
Avoid the Wolf River Trails near Shelby Farms Park. They are *still* closed due to flooding damage and ongoing assessment/debris removal. Do not attempt to run, walk, or bike on closed trails. This is a legitimate safety hazard.
</AlertBox>

### Pro Tip: Embrace the Treadmill (Sometimes)
