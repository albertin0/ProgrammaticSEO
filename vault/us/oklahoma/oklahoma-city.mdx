<AlertBox type="warning">
### Pollenocalypse Now!
If you're an allergy sufferer, consider this your official warning. High pollen counts can turn a great workout into a snot-filled struggle. Pop those antihistamines *before* you head out, not after you're already wheezing your way up the Lake Hefner trails. Keep an eye on itchy eyes and a runny nose; those are signs your body is fighting a losing battle.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
### Mind the Micro-Muck
That moderate AQI isn't going to kill you, but consistently breathing in particulate matter isn't exactly a recipe for peak performance or long-term lung health. If you're planning a high-intensity session, especially if you're already prone to respiratory issues, maybe dial it back or opt for an indoor sweat today.
</AlertBox>

## Trail Talk & Urban Obstacles

Good news for the dirt lovers: Bluff Creek Park trails are reportedly open to all users as of a few days ago, with folks on the ground suggesting they're in decent shape. Just be mindful of any lingering soft spots, especially after any recent moisture, and try to flatten out any ruts you create. Don't be "that guy" who makes the trail worse for everyone else.

Now, for those navigating the urban sprawl, prepare for the usual OKC traffic circus. The City of OKC's traffic advisories from February 23rd highlight ongoing road construction and utility work all over town, and ODOT recently issued another advisory on Feb 24th. That ongoing I-40 construction near the Tinker/240 interchange, which has been causing major delays, is still a thing. So, if your route takes you near major thoroughfares, factor in extra time for detours and a bit more exhaust fume exposure.

<AlertBox type="danger">
### Construction Chaos Continues
While not a direct "workout hazard," ongoing road construction can mean detours that push you onto less-safe routes, or force you to cross busier intersections. Always stay alert, assume drivers don't see you, and choose well-lit paths if you're out near dusk. The Oklahoma City metro is a perpetual construction zone, so plan your route.
</AlertBox>
