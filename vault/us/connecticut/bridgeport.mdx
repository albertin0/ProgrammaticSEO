## Today's Rundown

Today’s low pollen count is about the only truly good news for outdoor enthusiasts. Enjoy it while it lasts, because the rest of the forecast demands a healthy dose of caution.

The primary concern remains residual snow and ice. While major roads might be clear, any lesser-used routes, especially within our local parks or along the Pequonnock River Trail, are prime candidates for hidden hazards. That recent blizzard means plenty of frozen remnants are waiting to catch you off guard. Slip and falls aren't just annoying; they can lead to sprains, fractures, or worse. As a physical therapist, trust me, you do *not* want to spend your recovery wishing you'd worn those microspikes.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Watch for Black Ice on All Surfaces!** Even seemingly dry pavement can hide a clear, thin layer of ice, especially in shaded areas or on bridges. Short, shuffling steps are your friend. Assume it's icy until proven otherwise.
</AlertBox>

Then there's the air. An AQI of 72 isn't going to send you to the ER, but with fine particulate matter as the dominant pollutant, anyone with asthma, COPD, or even just sensitive lungs should be mindful. If you start feeling any irritation or shortness of breath, cut your workout short. This isn't the time to push through it for bragging rights.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Layer Up, Buttercup!** The clear skies are deceptive. Dress in moisture-wicking layers, including gloves, a hat, and a neck gaiter. You can always shed layers if you warm up, but you can't add what you didn't bring.
</AlertBox>

Finally, the PBR: Unleash The Beast event is happening today at the Total Mortgage Arena. While this is an indoor event and won't directly impact your trail run, expect increased traffic and potential parking headaches in the downtown area if you're planning to head that way. Just another reason to maybe stick to your local loops.
