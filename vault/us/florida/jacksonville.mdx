## The Rundown

Alright, Jacksonville, February 26th is serving up some classic Florida "nice-ish" weather. We've got clear skies and a comfortable 13.7°C (around 57°F) to start the day. Sounds great, right? Almost. Don't be fooled by the sunshine; there are a few things to keep your eyes peeled for.

### Air Quality & Your Lungs

The Air Quality Index is sitting at a moderate 71, with ozone as the dominant pollutant. Now, while this isn't the wildfire smoke we've been warned about (which can really mess with your lungs), ozone at this level can still irritate sensitive individuals. If you've got asthma or any other respiratory issues, take it easy, maybe shorten your outdoor session, or stick to less strenuous activities. It's not a stay-indoors day, but it's also not a "breathe deep, it's pristine!" kind of day either.

### That Persistent Fire Danger

This has been a recurring theme, and for good reason. Northeast Florida is *dry*. We've had a lot of freezes that killed off vegetation, and not enough rain to compensate. Today, those southwest winds are going to be gusting up to 24 mph inland. That's a bad combo. Elevated fire danger isn't just a headline; it means a flicked cigarette or an unattended bonfire (which, let's be honest, you shouldn't be having anyway without a permit) can quickly get out of control. If you're running trails, especially anything near the St. Johns River basin, be hyper-aware and report anything suspicious.

### Wind and Your Joints

Speaking of those winds, they aren't just a fire hazard. Gusts can be annoying for runners and cyclists, making you work harder and potentially throwing off your balance. If you're out on an exposed route, say along the Timucuan Trail, be ready for some serious headwind sections. It's not going to dislocate anything, but it might make your joints feel a little more stressed if you're pushing against it for too long. Pay attention to your form and don't fight it too hard.

## Local Hazards & Roadblocks

If you're planning a run downtown, know that the Hogan Street Link of the **Emerald Trail** is still under construction, with segments expected to open by summer 2026. This means potential detours or narrow pathways if you're trying to connect through that area. Similarly, the "Stadium of the Future" construction around **EverBank Stadium** is causing headaches, even for future events like the Gate River Run, with changes to routes and parking. So, if your loop usually takes you through those zones, assume there might be some rerouting or congestion. Best to scout ahead or have a backup plan.
