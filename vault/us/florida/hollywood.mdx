### Lungs & Air Quality

Your lungs generally get a break today with pollen levels staying low. You won't be wheezing through oak bloom, which is always a win in South Florida. However, the Air Quality Index is sitting at a moderate 64, with ozone being the dominant pollutant. That's not terrible, but it's not crystal-clear mountain air either. If you're sensitive or planning a super-high-intensity session, just be aware that ozone can cause some respiratory irritation, especially when you're sucking wind.

### Joints & Terrain

The good news for your joints is the comfortable temperature. No extreme heat to worry about, so hydration should be easier to manage, and your muscles will thank you for the mild conditions.

Now, for the reality check: **Hollywood Beach Broadwalk** is a bit of a war zone right now. Broward County's Dune and Beach Restoration Project is in full swing, and it's not a pretty sight for runners. Dump trucks and heavy equipment are moving sand from sunrise to sunset, Monday through Friday. This means temporary beach access closures at spots like Meade Street and Bougainvilla Terrace. If your usual route involves a smooth, uninterrupted run along the Broadwalk, expect detours, bottlenecks, and the general chaos that comes with large-scale construction.

We also have ongoing utility work along A1A from Van Buren to Jefferson Streets, which means single-lane closures. Again, not ideal for a zen-like run or bike ride. While there were red flag warnings for high fire danger and strong winds a few days ago (February 23rd) across South Florida, today's clear conditions suggest those immediate threats have passed. No recent flooding concerns to report either, which is always a relief in our neck of the woods.

So, if you're hitting the Broadwalk, manage your expectations. You're going to be dodging more than just slow walkers. Maybe head inland to a neighborhood park, or hit up West Lake Park if you're craving some nature trails without the heavy machinery soundtrack.
