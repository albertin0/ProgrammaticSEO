## The Rundown

Alright, let's talk turkey. It's a clear day, which is a blessing, especially if you're hitting the pavement early or late. The provided pollen data says "Low," so your allergies should stay quiet, a rare gift in South Florida. But before you lace up those new Hokas and declare it a perfect day, remember you're running in Fort Lauderdale, not some pristine mountain trail.

The air quality, sitting at an AQI of 65 with ozone as the dominant pollutant, puts us squarely in the "Moderate" category. While most of you iron-lunged warriors might barely notice, AccuWeather's still warning that sensitive groups might experience symptoms like throat irritation or difficulty breathing if you spend too much time outside. So, if you've got asthma or are just generally prone to wheezing, keep an eye on how you feel. It's not a red alert, but it's not pristine mountain air either.

Now, for you beach lovers or open water swimmers, listen up: **DO NOT get complacent in the ocean today.** There's a high rip current risk in effect for Coastal Broward County until at least 7:00 PM tonight. Those things are no joke and can drag even the strongest swimmers into deeper water faster than you can say "shark bait." Stay out of the water unless you're confident in your skills and sticking close to a lifeguard. Seriously, it's not worth the heroics.

### Construction Zone City

If you're planning a run or ride anywhere near downtown or along the waterfront, prepare for detours, dust, and general disruption. Fort Lauderdale is basically one giant construction site these days.

*   The second phase of the **Society Las Olas** apartment complex at 140 SW 2nd Street is still chugging along.
*   The **International Swimming Hall of Fame (ISHOF)** redevelopment down by the New River Sound means ongoing work on the Ocean Rescue Headquarters and seawall improvements.
*   And don't forget the shiny new **City Hall** that's going up at 100 N. Andrews Avenue, meaning more heavy machinery and blocked access points.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Heads up for pedestrian traffic around Las Olas!** There are ongoing lane and sidewalk closures for various mobility and stormwater projects all over the city. Case in point, the pedestrian sidewalk on southbound SE 6th Avenue, from Las Olas Boulevard to SE 2nd Street, is reportedly closed, with detours sending you down SE 3rd Avenue. Pay attention to signage, don't assume your usual route is clear, and watch out for distracted drivers trying to navigate the mess.
