### Weather & Air Quality: Don't Get Complacent

Look, the morning is gorgeous. You've got clear skies and a comfortable temperature that's practically begging you to get outside. But this is Albuquerque; things change faster than a politician's promises. While the AQI is currently sitting pretty at a "Good" 39 with inhalable particulate matter (&lt;10µm) as the dominant pollutant, don't let that lull you into a false sense of security for the days ahead.

The biggest physical challenge today, beyond your own internal whining, will be the wind. If your grand plan involves an evening stroll or a fast bike ride along the Paseo del Bosque Trail, be warned: those northwest winds are expected to pick up significantly tonight, hitting 20 to 25 mph with gusts possibly reaching 35 mph before midnight. That's enough to turn a pleasant pace into a full-body workout just trying to stay upright, and if you're cycling, consider it a full-contact sport.

### Allergies: It's Just Getting Started

For the allergy sufferers among us, congratulations! You've made it to late February, which means juniper pollen is already hitting "High." The overall pollen count might be "Moderate," but that doesn't mean your nose knows the difference. If you're susceptible, you're probably already feeling it. This is your life now until monsoon season. Stock up on tissues and antihistamines, because it's only going to get worse.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Pro Tip for Pollen Sufferers:** Hit the trails earlier in the morning before the sun really gets things stirred up, or wait until after a good evening breeze has cleared some of it out. And for the love of your lungs, shower and change clothes immediately when you get home to ditch those pollen particles.
</AlertBox>

### Trail Talk & Construction Chaos

No major *new* trail closures in the last 24 hours that will shut down your favorite routes. However, a friendly reminder that the ambitious (and seemingly never-ending) Albuquerque Rail Trail project is still a thing. Construction continues at various points, particularly around Central Avenue and First Street, aiming for some parts to open by early 2026, with overall completion years away. So, if your route usually takes you through Downtown or Old Town, keep an eye out for detours. It's a nice idea, but the execution is a marathon, not a sprint.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Wind Advisory for Exposed Trails:** Expect strong crosswinds and headwinds on exposed areas like the Paseo del Bosque Trail, especially closer to the Rio Grande and on open stretches. If you're a lighter runner or cyclist, this could legitimately knock you around. Keep your eyes peeled for debris.
</AlertBox>
