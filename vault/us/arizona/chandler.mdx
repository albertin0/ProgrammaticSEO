### Air Quality & Your Lungs

That "Moderate" AQI with PM10 as the dominant pollutant means there's enough junk floating around to potentially irritate your respiratory system, especially if you're pushing hard. You might not see it, but your lungs will feel it.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### **Particulate Matter Warning:**
With the AQI hitting 52-56 and driven by particulate matter, your lungs are going to be working overtime. If you have asthma, allergies, or any respiratory sensitivities, consider keeping your workout indoors today. Even healthy folks might feel a little extra burn or cough on intense efforts. Don't be a hero; listen to your body.
</AlertBox>

### Construction Zone Chaos

You know the drill. ADOT's still hammering away at widening Loop 202 Santan Freeway between Loop 101 and Val Vista Drive. Expect potential delays or detours if your route involves hitting any of the underpasses or overpasses in that stretch. They're doing drainage work and bridge widening, which often means unexpected closures or shifted pathways.

But more locally, the City of Chandler is working on protected bike lanes along Frye Road, specifically from west of Arizona Avenue all the way to the **Paseo Trail (Consolidated Canal)**. If you use that section of the Paseo Trail for your runs or rides, be prepared for sections to be completely blocked off, uneven surfaces, or heavy equipment. It's not a suggestion to be careful; it's a directive. Watch your step, watch for trucks, and assume drivers aren't looking out for you.

And just to add to the fun, repaving work is *starting* on Monday (March 2) on Chandler Boulevard at Delaware Street, which means even today, things could be getting prepped and traffic patterns are probably already a mess. This kind of work is notorious for creating uneven pavement, loose gravel, and sudden lane shifts—all ripe for a rolled ankle or worse.

<AlertBox type="danger">
### **Local Construction Hazard:**
The Frye Road protected bike lane project impacting the Paseo Trail near the Consolidated Canal is a real ankle-biter. Loose debris, unannounced detours, and heavy machinery are all on the menu. If you're running there, slow your roll, stay vigilant, and maybe opt for a different route to save yourself a trip to my office for a sprained ankle.
