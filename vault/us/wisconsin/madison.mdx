Look, the skies are clear, the sun's out, and you probably need to burn off that extra cheese curd consumption from last night. So yeah, you *can* work out outside today. But let's be realistic about what you're stepping into.

The temperature is hovering around 2.4°C, but with that typical Madison chill, it feels more like freezing. Your lungs will get a shock, and your joints will feel it for the first mile or two, especially if you're not properly warmed up. Don't be a hero trying to hit a PR without a solid 15 minutes of dynamic stretching. That's how my PT practice stays busy, and honestly, I'd rather see you out running than laid up on my table.

<AlertBox type="info">
### Dress for Success (and Survival)
Think layers. A good base, an insulating mid-layer, and a wind-resistant outer shell are your friends. Don't forget gloves, a hat, and probably a neck gaiter. Your extremities are the first to go.
</AlertBox>

Now, let's talk about the real joy of running in Madison: navigating our perpetually under-construction landscape. The **John Nolen Drive Reconstruction Project** is still chugging along like a slow-moving freight train, and it's going to be with us through 2026. While the bike path on the causeway is miraculously still open through "early summer 2026," traffic on John Nolen itself is down to one lane in each direction. If you're running or cycling near there, keep your head on a swivel. Drivers are already irritated, and their spatial awareness might be even worse than usual.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### John Nolen Drive Traffic & Path Users
Exercise extreme caution on or near John Nolen Drive. Distracted drivers combined with reduced lanes are a recipe for disaster. The bike path is open for now, but assume cars aren't looking for you.
</AlertBox>

Speaking of paths, if you're a fan of the **UW-Madison Lakeshore Path**, particularly the section near Memorial Union, be aware of ongoing reconstruction south of the Limnology building. Cyclists are being detoured to the north side of the building, and this route is now shared with pedestrians. It's a tighter squeeze, so be courteous and announce yourself if you're zipping by. Nobody wants to end up in Lake Mendota because someone wasn't paying attention.

The Air Quality Index is sitting at a "moderate" 72 today, with ozone being the dominant pollutant. For most of you, it's fine. For those with sensitive respiratory systems, or if you're planning an exceptionally high-intensity workout, just be aware it's not perfectly clean air out there.

Pollen's low, so at least your nose won't be running more than you are.
