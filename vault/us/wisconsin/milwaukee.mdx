## The Gory Details

Look, it's February in Milwaukee. If you're surprised by cold, you haven't lived here long enough. That -5.5°C feels colder when you're actually moving, especially with any wind that might sneak up on you along the lakefront. Your body will burn more calories just trying to stay warm, which sounds great until you realize it means you'll be gassed faster.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Beware the Frozen Paths:** Even if it's "clear," black ice is a real thing. The paths, especially those shaded areas on the Oak Leaf Trail or the sidewalks downtown, could be slick. Falling on ice is not going to do your knees or wrists any favors, and trust me, I've seen enough of those injuries in the clinic to last a lifetime. Shuffle, don't stride, if you're unsure.
</AlertBox>

The air quality, sitting at a moderate 72, means that if you're asthmatic or have any kind of respiratory issues, you're going to feel this. Cold air constricts airways, and those particulates just give it more to chew on. This isn't the day to try for a PR, unless your PR involves hacking up a lung afterwards.

And don't get me started on the construction. The I-94 East-West Freeway Project is a gift that keeps on taking, with significant closures everywhere. The 27th Street bridge over I-94 is closed through late 2026, and ramps off I-94 to 26th Street/St. Paul Avenue are also shut down until summer. If you were planning on navigating that mess on foot, you've got another thing coming. The detours are mostly for cars, which means you're often left dodging traffic or adding significant mileage to find an alternate route that doesn't feel like a death wish.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Dress Like a Local, Not a Tourist:** Layers are your friend. Think wicking base, insulating mid-layer, and a wind-resistant outer shell. Don't forget your extremities – good gloves, a hat that covers your ears, and wool socks are non-negotiable. Exposed skin gets frostbitten faster than you can say "Lake Michigan."
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Road Hazard Alert:** Multiple construction projects mean active work zones and traffic disruptions are widespread. Hawley Road at I-94 is fully closed through late 2028, for example. Assume drivers aren't looking out for you, because most of them aren't. Stick to known safe routes, if such a thing exists in this city right now.
</AlertBox>
