## Your Lungs Will Notice

Alright, so the pollen count is low, which is a win for anyone with seasonal sniffles. But don't get too comfortable. That AQI of 70, with PM10 as the dominant pollutant, means the air quality isn't exactly pristine. It's not "stay indoors and panic" bad, but it's enough to irritate sensitive airways, especially when you're sucking down lungfuls of it during a tempo run. Think of it as a low-grade annoyance your respiratory system would rather do without.

## Watch Your Step, Especially by the Lake

The weather apps are calling it "clear," but let's be real, it's Milwaukee in February. That 0.3°C is the sweet spot for turning any residual moisture into an invisible skating rink. And here's the kicker: WUWM just reported on "unusual" Lake Michigan ice today. Unusual in February can mean anything from spectacular formations to unstable, deadly conditions. Stay far, far away from venturing onto it, and be extremely cautious on any paths immediately adjacent to the shoreline.

<AlertBox type="danger">
### DANGER: Lake Michigan Ice and Black Ice
Reports indicate unusual ice conditions on Lake Michigan today. DO NOT approach or attempt to walk/run on any ice near the shoreline. Furthermore, with temperatures right around freezing, watch for black ice on all paved surfaces, especially in shaded areas or on bridges. A slip and fall on frozen concrete can put you in my PT clinic for months.
</AlertBox>

## Trail Updates & Detour Frustrations

If your route involves the **Hank Aaron State Trail**, be advised: sections are still a mess. The River Loop is closed through winter 2026 for the 16th Street bridge replacement. And another chunk, specifically at 32nd Street and Canal Street, is also shut down until June 2026 due to the ongoing I-94 project, with a detour available on Canal Street.

On top of that, the infamous I-94 East-West project's "Early East Leg" construction kicked off earlier this month. This means the 27th Street Bridge over I-94 is closed long-term, and parts of St. Paul Avenue are impacted. So, if you're typically navigating those west side routes, expect delays and have a backup plan.

<AlertBox type="info">
### Plan Your Route Wisely
Check the Milwaukee County Parks website for real-time trail closures, especially around the Hank Aaron State Trail and any routes crossing or paralleling I-94. Assume your usual path is interrupted and be ready to improvise.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Bundle Up Against the Wind
Even with clear skies, those 10-20 mph southerly gusts, particularly by the lake, will make 0.3°C feel significantly colder. Layer up, protect your ears and face, and consider wind-resistant outer shells to avoid unnecessary discomfort or even frostnip.
</AlertBox>

In short, it's a day for the dedicated, the well-informed, and the well-layered. Don't be a hero, be smart.
