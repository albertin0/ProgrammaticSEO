The primary hazard today is the sheer cold, compounded by hard-packed and potentially icy trails. This is prime time for frostbite if you're not adequately covered, and hypothermia is a real threat with prolonged exposure.
</AlertBox>

## AQI Breakdown: What You're Breathing
The AQI is 71, flagged by "Inhalable particulate matter (&lt;10µm)." This means there are tiny particles floating around that can get into your lungs. For most healthy people, it's just a nuisance, but if you have asthma or other respiratory issues, you might notice it. Don't go setting any personal bests today.

## Gear Up, Seriously
If you absolutely *must* get outside, layer up like you're heading to the South Pole. Think thermal base layers, insulating mid-layers, and a windproof, waterproof outer shell. Don't forget your face, head, and hands. Balaclava, warm hat, mittens over gloves. Traction devices on your shoes are non-negotiable for any icy patches. And hydrate, even if you don't feel thirsty in the cold.
</AlertBox>
