The National Weather Service in Anchorage issued a Cold Weather Advisory until noon AKST today, warning of very cold wind chills as low as 20 to 30 below zero. This is not the day for heroics, folks. ### Trail Talk

The Nordic Skiing Association of Anchorage reported that trails like Kincaid Park's lighted loops were groomed as of yesterday, but with a warning: "It's noticeably colder out there today... Bundle up, manage expectations, and enjoy your time on the snow." "Manage expectations" means don't expect PRs when your lungs are fighting for every breath in sub-zero temps. Those trails might be groomed, but the snow will be hard and icy in this kind of cold, making for a different kind of challenge. If you insist on cross-country skiing, ensure every inch of skin is covered. Seriously, every inch.

<AlertBox type="warning">
#### Watch Your Downtown Routes!
It's Fur Rondy! Downtown Anchorage is bustling with activities from today through March 8th. If you planned a run or walk through the city center, be aware of crowds, potential road closures for events, and general congestion. Stick to designated pedestrian areas and be hyper-aware of your surroundings. And for the love of all that is holy, drivers, remember that pedestrian deaths are on the rise in Anchorage. Slow down.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
#### Layer Smart, Stay Hydrated (Yes, Even When It's Freezing)
If you're heading out, even for a short dog walk, layer up. We're talking base layers, mid-layers, windproof outer shells. Balaclava, goggles, heavy mittens, and insulated boots are non-negotiable. And drink water *before* you go out. Dehydration happens faster in the cold than you think.
</AlertBox>
