While the skies are clear and the sun might even make a cameo, the groundwork is treacherous. This isn't just about a bit of slush; the city has been blanketed with over three feet of snow, and the cleanup is an ongoing battle.

<AlertBox type="danger">
#### **Icy, Hazardous Conditions Persist City-Wide**
Despite the clear weather, Providence is deep in snow recovery. Roads are still constricted, snow banks are towering, and hidden ice patches are a massive fall risk. Sidewalks are largely uncleared or uneven. Unless you fancy a trip to the ER with a broken bone or severe sprain, keep your run indoors. Your joints will thank you. The city is literally in "phase 2" of snow removal, which means making roads *usable*, not pristine.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
#### **Moderate Air Quality - Ozone Dominant**
The Air Quality Index is sitting at 68, which is in the moderate range, with ozone as the primary pollutant. For most, it's not a showstopper, but if you have respiratory issues, or if you're planning a high-intensity workout, you might notice some irritation. Combined with the other hazards, it's just another reason to pump the brakes.
</AlertBox>

#### **Stay Flexible, Stay Safe**

Or, you know, do some burpees in your living room. No one's judging (much).
