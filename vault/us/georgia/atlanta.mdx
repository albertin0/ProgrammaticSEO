## Lungs: The Ozone is Hanging Around

Your nose isn't running from pollen today – that count is blessedly low, a rare win for February in Georgia. So, allergy sufferers, rejoice. However, the Air Quality Index is sitting at a moderate 80, with ozone being the dominant pollutant. For most of us, this is just "eh, could be better." But for anyone with asthma, COPD, or generally sensitive airways, a moderate AQI is a polite warning to maybe scale back the intensity. AccuWeather even calls an AQI of 80 "poor" and "unhealthy for sensitive groups," which sounds about right for how my lungs feel after a long run on an "acceptable" air day. Don't be a hero and push it if you feel tightness or shortness of breath. You're trying to improve your health, not land yourself in urgent care.

## Joints & Paths: A Minefield of Misery

Temperature-wise, 15.6°C (60°F) is damn near perfect for a run – cool enough not to overheat, warm enough not to freeze your fingers off. However, that "clear" condition you might see out your window could be short-lived. The forecast indicates heavy rain is on its way, with a 90% chance today. That means all those roots and rocks on the unpaved sections of the South Peachtree Creek Trail at Mason Mill Park are going to be slicker than fresh ice. If you're heading out, plan for wet conditions underfoot, which increases your risk of a slip, a twisted ankle, or a face plant.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Beltline Blues: Construction Ahead
If you're eyeing the Atlanta Beltline, particularly the Southside Trail, be aware that various segments are still under construction. Segments 4 and 5 had their opening delayed yet again due to recent rain impacting concrete work. That means railings might not be up, surfaces might be unfinished, and general access could be a headache. The United Avenue ramp? Still closed for construction, even when the rest opens up. You'll find ongoing wall construction and grading on Segments 2 and 3 as well. Don't expect a smooth, uninterrupted ride or run. You'll likely encounter detours, uneven ground, and active work zones.
</AlertBox>

### Road Closures & Detours

It's not just the trails. The City of Atlanta’s Department of Transportation has some road work that might impact your run if you're hitting the streets:

*   **10th Street NE** has a 24-hour closure for sewer mainline rehabilitation, which started early February and is expected to last about two weeks. Prepare for detours in that area.
*   **26th Street** is closed for five days for curb and asphalt restoration.
*   One southbound lane on **Spring Street (US 19/GA 9)** is being repurposed for a bike lane, along with other upgrades, leading to daily closures until late March.
