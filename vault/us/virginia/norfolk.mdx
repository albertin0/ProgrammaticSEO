### Lungs: Breathe Easy? Not Exactly.

While the pollen count is happily sitting at "Low" – a win, I guess – our air quality is less than stellar. The AQI is pegged at 74, officially "Moderate," but don't let that fool you. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and Nitrogen Dioxide are the main culprits, and some reports are calling it "Poor" for sensitive folks. Long-term exposure to this stuff isn't doing your respiratory system any favors. If you're planning on pushing hard on the Elizabeth River Trail, especially near any busy roads or industrial areas, maybe dial it back a notch. Or consider an indoor spin class where the air is, you know, filtered.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Moderate Air Quality Alert:** Our air quality is acceptable for most, but if you've got asthma, COPD, or just generally enjoy breathing, consider reducing prolonged or heavy exertion outdoors. Keep an eye on those PM2.5 levels.
</AlertBox>

### Joints: Bundle Up or Bust

The temperature might hit a milder 56°F later, but this morning is cold. We're talking 39.56°F (4.2°C) and clear skies, which often means that biting chill gets right into your bones. Don't skip the warm-up, and for the love of all that is holy, wear layers. Your knees, especially, will thank you for not sending them out unprotected into the cold. We haven't had any recent cold weather advisories, but common sense still applies.

### Local Hazards & Annoyances

Beyond the air, there's the usual Norfolk chaos. If you were planning on driving on I-64 East this morning to hit up the trails around the Virginia Wesleyan area, think again. There was a multi-vehicle crash near the Tidewater Drive exit that shut down all eastbound lanes and caused about two miles of traffic backup. This was late last night, so expect residual headaches for a bit.

Construction around the city, particularly with the ongoing Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel Expansion Project, always means potential lane closures and detours. While nothing major impacting *foot* traffic on trails was reported in the last 24 hours, remember that Metronet and Virginia Natural Gas also have various projects causing intermittent lane and sidewalk closures. Stay vigilant, especially if you're navigating urban sections like those around Llewellyn Avenue.

No active flooding alerts for Norfolk, which is a minor miracle for us. But let's be real, it's February, and the ground can still be soft in spots, especially after any recent precipitation. If you're hitting up any unpaved sections of the Elizabeth River Trail, watch your footing.
