### The Rundown: Don't Be a Hero, Just Be Smart

Look, it's 10.8°C (that's 51°F for you Celsius-averse folks) and the sky is clear. On paper, it's a perfectly acceptable day to pound the pavement or hit the trails. Your seasonal allergies shouldn't be flaring up with a low pollen count, so that's one less thing to blame for your mediocre pace.

However, let's talk about that Air Quality Index. An AQI of 77 means it's "Moderate," with ozone as the dominant pollutant. What does "moderate" mean for a runner? It means it's not great, especially if you're pushing hard or have any respiratory issues. You're essentially inviting a little extra irritation into your lungs. Your body works harder to process that junk, even if you don't feel it immediately. So, maybe don't go for your longest, hardest run today, especially during peak afternoon hours when ozone tends to be higher. Save the PR attempt for a day when the air isn't trying to slow you down.
