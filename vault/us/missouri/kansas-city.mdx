## The Rundown: Your KC Workout for February 28, 2026

If you're looking for an outdoor sweat session today, you're in luck, mostly. We've got clear skies and a comfortable 9.3°C (around 49°F) in Kansas City, making it feel pretty darn good for late February. Pollen levels are low, which is a win for anyone usually battling seasonal sniffles.

However, let's not get complacent. Our Air Quality Index (AQI) is clocking in at 68, which is "Moderate," with inhalable particulate matter as the dominant pollutant. For most healthy folks, you'll probably be fine, but if you're prone to respiratory issues, or if you're pushing serious mileage, you might feel it. Yesterday was actually "Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups", so today is an improvement, but still warrants a bit of caution. Don't go sucking down lungfuls of exhaust next to the downtown traffic on Grand.

### Trail Talk & Urban Adventures

Big news for trail enthusiasts: the bridge over MO 350 near Noland Road on the **Rock Island Trail** officially reopened yesterday (Friday, Feb 27) around 5 PM! It's a wider, new-and-improved bridge, which is fantastic. Just keep an eye out for minor temporary modifications as the larger MO 350 construction project continues.

Now, for those thinking about hitting the unpaved paths, like some sections of the Blue River Park trails, you need to use your head. Many urban dirt trails are still in that frustrating freeze/thaw cycle. What looks solid in the morning might turn into a muddy, rutted mess by midday. If you're leaving ruts or sliding around, you're damaging the trail. Stay off it. Urban Trail Co. is pretty clear: "Use ONLY when 100% FROZEN! leave a mark and leave the park!" Seriously, don't be that person. Stick to the paved routes or check specific trail conditions on apps like RainoutLine.

If your route takes you through the urban core, especially around the **18th & Vine Jazz District** or the Paseo, be aware that the "Revive the Vine" initiative still has active construction zones. 18th Street between The Paseo and Woodland Avenue is closed to traffic, and you might find lane closures on 19th Street and Lydia Avenue near the new parking garage project, expected through May. Pedestrian sidewalks are open but might not be fully connected at intersections. Stay alert and watch for construction vehicles.
