<AlertBox type="warning">
### Don't Skimp on Layers, Folks!
With temperatures dipping below zero and wind chills making it feel even colder, hypothermia is a real concern if you're not dressed right. Think layers, proper winter running gear, and covering all exposed skin. Fingers and toes are usually the first to go, so invest in good gloves and thick socks.
</AlertBox>

### Trail Talk: Gatineau Park Conditions

Look, it's winter in Quebec. You know what that means. The trails in Gatineau Park, while open and generally recommended, will have snow. This means uneven surfaces, potential icy patches underneath that fresh powder, and overall trickier footing. If you're planning a run or a serious hike, microspikes or a good pair of trail shoes with aggressive tread are pretty much mandatory unless you enjoy unplanned acrobatics. Nobody needs a sprained ankle just trying to enjoy the scenery.

<AlertBox type="info">
### Eyes on the AQI
While "moderate" isn't "danger," that fine particulate matter can still irritate your respiratory system, especially when you're breathing hard in cold air. If you have asthma or other lung conditions, consider opting for an indoor workout today. Listen to your body – it's smarter than your Strava stats.
</AlertBox>
