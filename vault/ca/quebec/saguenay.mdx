## Saguenay's Winter Running Realities

Let's talk about that air. An AQI of 72 means "Moderate" for most folks. For us runners, though, "moderate" ozone can feel like someone's got a small, invisible straw trying to suck the oxygen out of your lungs mid-tempo. It's not a red flag to bail entirely, but if you're planning a hard interval session or a long run, maybe dial back the intensity a notch. Your lungs will thank you, and your future self (the one who wants to keep running without an inhaler) will appreciate it too.

Pollen is low, which is fantastic for those of us who usually spend spring looking like we've cried through a rom-com marathon. So, no excuses there.

### Local Construction Nuisances

Now, for the local fun. If your route takes you anywhere near **R-172 at the height of Rue Bayard in Chicoutimi**, be aware. There are ongoing public utility works causing traffic slowdowns and potential obstructions for pedestrians and cyclists. It's "durée indéterminée," which in local parlance means "it'll be done when it's done, don't ask." So, if you're planning to crisscross that area, maybe scope out an alternative or add a few minutes to your usual pace to navigate the mess. Nobody needs to be dodging backhoes while trying to hit their splits. The Ville de Saguenay's traffic map is your friend for these kinds of things, if you can tolerate municipal websites.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Dress for the Arctic, not just "chilly."** -8.3°C with any wind will penetrate layers quickly. Think technical base layers, a good mid-layer, and a wind-resistant outer shell. Don't forget insulated gloves or mitts, a warm hat that covers your ears, and a neck gaiter to protect your face and warm the air you breathe. Frostbite on exposed skin is no joke, and it happens faster than you think.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
**Warm up gradually, cool down thoroughly.** In these temperatures, your muscles are tight. Start with dynamic stretches and a very easy jog before picking up the pace. After your run, get inside and out of those sweaty clothes as quickly as possible to prevent hypothermia. Stretch indoors.
</AlertBox>

Overall, today isn't the worst day to hit the trails or roads, but it demands respect. Gear up properly, be smart about your routes, and listen to your body, especially your lungs.
