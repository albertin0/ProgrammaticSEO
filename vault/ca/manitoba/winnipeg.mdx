Today is absolutely NOT the day to pretend you're a polar bear, unless your idea of "working out" involves rapid shivering indoors.

<LungsJointsScore score={2} />

<BulletList>
*   **Arctic Blast is no joke:** Temperatures are hovering around -25°C, but with the wind chill, it feels like a brutal -34°C to -40°C in parts of southern Manitoba, including Winnipeg. This isn't just "brisk," it's straight-up dangerous.
*   **Blowing Snow Hiding Hazards:** Strong northwest winds, gusting up to 80 km/h, are whipping up any loose snow, creating whiteout conditions and significantly reducing visibility. You won't see that patch of ice or hidden curb until you're on it, and good luck finding your way back if you venture far.
*   **Lungs Will Hate You:** While the AQI is only moderately high at 77 with ozone as the dominant pollutant, inhaling that cold, dry air combined with any irritants is a recipe for a burning throat and lungs.
</BulletList>

Forget your long run along the Nestaweya River Trail today. Seriously, just don't. The city is in the grip of an unseasonably cold arctic air mass, and while the skies might be clear, that's just a cruel trick of the light making you think it's a pleasant winter day. It's not. Environment and Climate Change Canada has been warning about these plunging temperatures and dangerous wind chills, which are expected to persist through the weekend. Yesterday saw wind gusts up to 83 km/h, causing blowing snow and treacherous travel conditions, and while highways might be "back open," that doesn't mean your face won't freeze off if you're out there.

If you absolutely *must* step outside, make it quick. Think sprinting to the mailbox, not a leisurely jog. Your exposed skin can freeze in minutes in these conditions. And don't even think about pushing a hard effort; your cardiovascular system is already working overtime just to keep you warm.

<AlertBox type="danger">
### Extreme Cold Warning: Frostbite Risk is High
With wind chill values dropping to -34°C and colder, exposed skin can develop frostbite in a matter of minutes. Layers, layers, layers, and absolutely no exposed skin. Cover your face, ears, and neck.
</AlertBox>

Even if you're just walking the dog around your neighbourhood streets, be wary of slippery patches hidden by blowing snow. The Residential Parking Ban is still in effect until 7 p.m. tonight, indicating ongoing street clearing efforts that might leave uneven surfaces or snowdrifts.

The low pollen count is the only good news today, but honestly, who cares about allergens when your eyeballs might freeze?

### Visibility Hazards
Gusty winds and blowing snow are causing widespread reductions in visibility, especially in open areas like parks or along the Red River. Be aware of rapidly changing conditions and potential whiteouts.
