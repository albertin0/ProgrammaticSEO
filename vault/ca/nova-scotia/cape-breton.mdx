### Don't Be a Hero, Cape Breton

Look, I get it. You've got the winter running gear, you've been cooped up, and a clear sky after a week of whatever the hell that was feels like an invitation. It's not. It's a test of your common sense, and frankly, most of you fail it regularly.

The ambient temperature is sitting at a crisp -9.2°C, but the wind chill will make that feel significantly colder, easily dropping into the -12°C to -15°C range. Your lungs are not designed for that. Prolonged exposure to air this cold can irritate your airways, leading to broncho-constriction – basically, your air tubes clamping down. If you've got asthma, you're looking at a bad time. Even if you don't, you'll be hacking up a lung and questioning all your life choices by the time you hit the **Skyline Trail** head.

Then there's the AQI. An 82, with ozone leading the charge, is squarely in the "Moderate" category. For those of us who actually care about our long-term health, moderate means "don't push it." Ozone at ground level is a lung irritant. Combine that with freezing air, and you're giving your respiratory system a double dose of punishment. This isn't some corporate wellness blog telling you to "listen to your body" with a smile; this is me, your resident PT, telling you to listen to your body because I don't want to see you in my clinic next week with a chest cold or worse.
