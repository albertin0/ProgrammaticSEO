## The Air: Not Exactly Champagne & Roses

Let's talk about that air. The AQI is sitting at 78, with ozone being the dominant pollutant. That's not "stay indoors and hermetically seal yourself" bad, but it's certainly not "breathe deep and feel invigorated" good either. If you’ve got sensitive lungs, or you’re planning an intense session, you might notice it. Your body works harder when the air quality is mediocre, especially when it’s cold.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Moderate AQI Alert:** With an AQI of 78 and Ozone as the main culprit, sensitive individuals might experience respiratory symptoms. Consider dialing back intensity or shortening your outdoor time.
</AlertBox>

## Weather: Winter's Last Gasp (Hopefully)

It’s -1.8°C and clear as a bell, which sounds lovely until you remember that -1.8°C is still *freezing*. Highs today are barely touching zero, and overnight lows are dipping to -6°C. That means cold hands, cold feet, and potentially icy patches in shady spots, especially if there was any melt yesterday. Clear skies are nice for visibility, but they don't magically make the air warm. Think layers, proper gloves, and a hat. Don't be that hero in shorts; nobody's impressed, and you're just risking a head cold.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Dress for the Chill:** Layer up! Even though it's clear, the temperature is below freezing. Proper gloves, hats, and moisture-wicking layers are essential to prevent hypothermia and frostbite.
</AlertBox>

## Trail Talk: Closed for Business (Sort Of)

If your usual routine involves escaping into nature, you might be out of luck at some of the popular spots. Conservation Halton has been doing hazardous tree removal, and it means **Mount Nemo's North Loop trail is currently closed**. Likewise, if you're headed to Rattlesnake Point, be aware that sections of the trails might be periodically inaccessible through March 6th due to more tree work. This isn't just a suggestion; it's for your safety (and the safety of the folks doing the chainsaw dance). Respect the closures, people. There are other trails, or stick to the paved stuff, but don't go trying to dodge falling timber. The Crawford Lake Boardwalk is also out of commission until summer, which, let's be honest, is a pretty long time to wait for a stroll.

As for hitting the road, there are some ongoing watermain replacement projects on Waterdown Road, Flatt Road, and Ireson Road. You might also encounter local closures on Millar Crescent, Autumn Hill Crescent, and Rockwood Drive due to various construction projects. Nothing major city-wide, but be mindful if your route takes you through these residential areas.

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Trail Closures at Conservation Halton Parks:** The North Loop trail at Mount Nemo is closed. Sections of Rattlesnake Point trails may be periodically inaccessible due to hazardous tree removal. DO NOT bypass closure signs. Find an alternate route.
</AlertBox>
