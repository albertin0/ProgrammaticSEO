## Hamilton's Trail Troubles & Air Quality Check

Let's be real, after the last few weeks of whatever winter decided to throw at us, 7 degrees and clear skies feels like a gift from the running gods. And with pollen levels staying low, your sniffles should be kept to a minimum, which is always a win when you're trying to push past the usual Staircase loop up the Escarpment.

However, it's not all rainbows and PRs. While the City of Hamilton cancelled its Cold Weather Alert on February 24th, indicating we're out of the deep freeze, that doesn't mean all the nastiness from winter has magically disappeared. We're sitting at an AQI of 71, with Ozone as the main troublemaker. For most, this "moderate" rating won't be a huge deal, but if you're asthmatic or have other respiratory issues, you might feel it. Pay attention to your body and maybe don't go for that all-out sprint if your chest feels tight.

<AlertBox type="warning">
  **Moderate Air Quality:** An AQI of 71 means the air quality is moderate. Sensitive groups, especially those with respiratory conditions, should consider reducing prolonged or heavy exertion outdoors. It's not a full-blown smog alert, but it's not pristine either.
</AlertBox>

And now for the trails. Hamilton Conservation Authority has been busy posting alerts, and you'd be wise to check their site before lacing up. Over at **Fifty Point Conservation Area**, the south-east section of the trail by the pond is closed due to "hazard trees" as of February 27th. Nobody needs a tree branch for a souvenir. Similarly, if you're planning on venturing into the beautiful **Dundas Valley Conservation Area**, be aware that private contractors are doing work on Martin's Road all month for the Mineral Springs Detention Facility. That means heavy equipment noise and presence on Martin's Road, which might interrupt your serene run on surrounding trails like Headwaters or Hilltop. Plus, even with the warmer temps, some of those shadier, less-traveled paths in the Dundas Valley could still have lingering icy patches from earlier in the month, so watch your footing.

<AlertBox type="info">
  **Trail Smart:** Always check the Hamilton Conservation Authority's website for specific trail closures and conditions before heading out, especially for areas like Fifty Point, Dundas Valley, or Valens Lake. Construction and hazard trees are real, folks.
</AlertBox>

Speaking of construction, if you're a fan of **Valens Lake**, a new campground loop is being added, so expect construction equipment and activity. More significantly, the boardwalk crossing the north side of Valens Lake is closed until March 31st for replacement. So, no scenic boardwalk strolls or runs there for a while.

So, while the weather's saying "go for it," the local conditions are saying "be smart." Enjoy the break from the deep freeze, but keep your head up and your eyes peeled.
