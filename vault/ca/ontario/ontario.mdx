Let's talk air. The Air Quality Index is at 68, which is "Moderate." The dominant pollutant today is Nitrogen dioxide. Now, for those of you who frequently pound the pavement along Archibald Ave or near the 60 Freeway, you're essentially getting a side of exhaust with your workout. While not in the "danger zone," sustained heavy breathing in moderate air quality, especially with traffic-related pollutants, isn't doing your lungs any favors. Think about those long runs, and how much air you're pulling in. Maybe keep the speed work for another day, or find some quieter, less congested routes.

**Cold Weather Risks Are Real:** That -2.7°C temperature means dressing in layers is non-negotiable. Exposed skin can get frostnip quicker than you think, and cold muscles are tight muscles, inviting strains and pulls. Hydrate, even if you don't feel thirsty, because the cold can be deceiving.

### Mountain Trails: Still a Winter Wonderland (Sort Of)

Remember those recent trail closures up in the Angeles National Forest, specifically around the Mount Baldy area, which includes our very own Ontario Peak Trail? While the official closure for hazardous winter conditions supposedly ended on February 23rd, let's not be naive. A couple of days isn't enough to melt away all the snow, ice, and black ice, especially on shaded trails or at higher elevations.

If you're eyeing a challenging ascent, understand that those trails might still be slick and dangerous. Falling on ice is a surefire way to end up in my office with a sprained ankle or worse. A "clear" sky doesn't mean a clear trail when you're talking about mountain conditions after a significant cold snap. Exercise extreme caution or, better yet, stick to lower-elevation paths until we get some consistent warmth.

<AlertBox type="info">
**Layer Up, Buttercup:** Seriously, multiple thin layers are your friend. You can always shed a layer if you warm up, but you can't conjure one out of thin air when you're shivering. Don't forget a hat, gloves, and a good base layer that wicks sweat.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Avoid High Mountain Trails:** Even though the official closure for areas like the Ontario Peak Trail *ended*, expect lingering ice and potentially dangerous conditions at elevation. Don't take unnecessary risks just because the calendar says it's technically open. Your orthopedic surgeon will thank you.
</AlertBox>

So, if you're heading out, be smart, stay warm, and don't push it too hard with that moderate AQI. Your body will thank you for being a little less hardcore today.
