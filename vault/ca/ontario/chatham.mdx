### Your Lungs & Joints Don't Lie: What to Watch For

The "clear" weather might fool you, but don't be a hero in shorts and a t-shirt. Today's wind chill is no joke. The persistent cold, coupled with the moderate AQI from those tiny particles floating around, means your lungs are working overtime to warm and filter the air you're sucking in. For your joints, cold muscles and connective tissue are stiffer and more prone to injury if you don't warm up thoroughly. Think of it as running with old, ungreased gears – things can snap.

Exposed skin is at risk for frostbite. Layer up, cover all extremities, and seriously, consider that neck gaiter.
