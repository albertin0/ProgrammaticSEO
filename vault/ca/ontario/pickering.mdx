### Pickering's Playground: What You Need to Know

Look, I get it. We runners are a stubborn bunch. "Clear skies!" you'll say, even if it's 3 degrees and the wind could peel paint. But seriously, today demands some smart choices.

The good news? Pollen is low, so at least your sinuses won't be staging a rebellion.

But let's talk about those closures. The fact that a significant section of our beloved Waterfront Trail, particularly around Rouge Beach and the pedestrian bridge over the Rouge River, is closed for *years* is a major headache. Don't assume you can just "squeeze by." Construction zones are active, and your safety isn't worth shaving a few kilometres off your long run. If your usual route takes you through there, you need an alternative. The City of Pickering has been kind enough to share this notice, but Parks Canada is running the show, and they're not messing around with access.

Beyond the Waterfront, there's also ongoing overnight utility relocation and drilling happening on **Pickering Parkway near Brock Road**. While this is mostly after hours (11:30 PM to 5:00 AM), if you're an insomniac runner or an early bird, be aware of increased noise and potential for construction traffic. Also, Beachpoint Park is temporarily closed due to plumbing issues. So, if you were planning a loop through there, reroute.
