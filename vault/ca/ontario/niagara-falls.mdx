### The Cold, Hard Truth About Your Lungs

Running in sub-zero temperatures, especially with recent wind chills that extreme, is no joke for your respiratory system. Your body has to work overtime to warm and humidify that dry, frigid air before it hits your delicate lung tissue. When it fails, you get that burning sensation, or worse, exercise-induced bronchoconstriction. Add in a "moderate" AQI for ozone, and you're essentially choosing to irritate your airways twice over. Ozone is an oxidant; it messes with your lung lining. Cold air does too. Combine them, and you're on a fast track to feeling like you just ran through a smoke detector testing facility. Keep your efforts low, or stay inside. Seriously.

### Ice, Ice Baby – But Not the Fun Kind

While recent localized flooding warnings from the Niagara Peninsula Conservation Authority technically expired a few days ago, don't assume every path is dry and clear. With the current temperatures, any residual moisture, especially in shaded areas or near the water's edge on the Niagara River Recreation Trail, will be a sheet of pure, unadulterated ice. A slip and fall on ice is a surefire way to turn your workout into an ER visit for a sprained ankle, fractured wrist, or even worse, a head injury. Nobody wants to explain *that* to their insurance.
