<AlertBox type="danger">
#### Beware of Frostbite & Hypothermia!
Temperatures this low, especially with any wind chill, can lead to frostbite in exposed skin in a surprisingly short amount of time. We're talking about feeling like -13°C, possibly colder. Cover every inch: good gloves, a hat, a neck gaiter, and wind-resistant layers are your best friends. Keep your outdoor sessions shorter than usual and listen to your body.
</AlertBox>

### Trail Talk: Tread Carefully, Brampton

Those picturesque trails, like sections of the Etobicoke Creek Trail system, might look inviting under a clear sky, but I guarantee they're harboring patches of ice and compacted snow. Even though the Centennial Park Trail Protection Project completed last spring, don't assume every part of every trail is perfectly cleared. The City and Region do their best with winter maintenance on paths like those in Chinguacousy Park, but they can't be everywhere at once. Slow down, shorten your stride, and if you're serious about traction, consider throwing on some microspikes. Your knees and ankles will thank you when you don't end up face-planting on a hidden ice sheet.

<AlertBox type="warning">
#### Construction Chaos Continues
If your route takes you anywhere near Hurontario Street, prepare for significant delays and potential pedestrian detours. The Hurontario LRT project is a beast that's been dragging on, making a mess of traffic and walkways. Even if you're just trying to get *to* your workout spot, factor in extra time and frustration. Check the City of Brampton's road closures map before you head out, because getting there can be half the battle.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
#### Hydration is Still Key!
Just because it's cold doesn't mean you can skip the water. Your body still loses fluids through sweat and respiration in cold, dry air. Stay hydrated before, during, and after your workout.
</AlertBox>
