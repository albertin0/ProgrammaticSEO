### Today's Rundown: Clear Skies, Cold Reality

So, you woke up and saw some actual sun and a relatively balmy 5.2°C? Cherish it. That's about the best you're going to get for a while. The "clear" condition from your data might be technically accurate for *right now*, but every other weather source is screaming "winter is coming, again." Expect things to go from zero to hero (or rather, zero to frigid) once the sun dips. We're talking snow showers by tonight and a harsh drop to -15°C. This isn't just a "grab a jacket" situation; this is "potential black ice on every unplowed path" territory.

Pollen is low, so your sinuses can relax, which is nice. The air quality, though, is a bit like a bad relationship – it's complicated. Your provided data suggests a moderate AQI of 70 with ozone as the dominant pollutant, which generally means "go for it." But then AccuWeather chimes in, warning of a "poor" AQI of 104 due to fine particulate matter (PM2.5), suggesting it's "unhealthy for sensitive groups." Meanwhile, Environment Canada is chilling with a "Low Risk" (AQHI 3). If you've got sensitive lungs, maybe dial it back or stick to shorter, less intense efforts. For everyone else, keep an eye on how you feel.
