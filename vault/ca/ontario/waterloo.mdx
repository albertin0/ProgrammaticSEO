Remember that 5 to 10 cm of snow we got between Tuesday night and Wednesday morning? Well, it's still out there. And with temperatures dipping below freezing, whatever melted during the slightly warmer parts of yesterday has likely refrozen into treacherous patches of ice. The city might have a handle on major roads, but side streets, park paths, and popular spots like sections of the Laurel Trail are probably slick. You're not just risking a twisted ankle; you're risking a full-on wipeout. Slow down, shuffle, and wear proper traction if you absolutely must be outside.

### Air Quality Isn't Helping Your Lungs

The Air Quality Index is sitting at a moderate 74, with ozone being the dominant pollutant. When you're huffing and puffing in cold air, your airways are already a bit constricted. Adding moderate ozone to the mix isn't going to make your run feel any easier or healthier. If you've got any kind of respiratory sensitivity, consider sticking to an indoor workout today. Give your lungs a break.
