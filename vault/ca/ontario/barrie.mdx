Don't even *think* about setting any personal bests out there today, Barrie. Seriously, save it for a day where Mother Nature isn't actively trying to hinder your progress with a flurry-filled slap to the face.

<LungsJointsScore score={4} />

<BulletList>
*   **Blowing Snow Advisory:** Environment Canada has issued a special weather statement for flurries, heavy at times, and *local blowing snow* hitting us this afternoon. Expect winds gusting up to 50 km/h, which means near-zero visibility in spots. That's not a charming scenic run; that's asking for trouble.
*   **Slippery Surfaces & Accumulation:** Those flurries aren't just for show. We're looking at 5 to 10 cm of new snow accumulation, making roads and walkways difficult to navigate. Combine that with the already massive snow banks from earlier this winter — some paths are already narrowed significantly, thanks to over 300 cm of accumulation we've seen since November.
*   **Moderate Air Quality:** While pollen is low, our AQI is sitting at a moderate 74, with ozone as the dominant pollutant. It's not a lung-buster, but it's not doing you any favours either when you're sucking wind.
</BulletList>

Look, I get it. You want to hit the pavement, or maybe the hard-packed snow, along the **North Shore Trail** by Kempenfelt Bay. And usually, I'd say go for it. But between the new snow coming down, the wind whipping off the bay, and the ongoing construction projects along the waterfront that *might* throw a wrench in your plans with intermittent closures (even if they're not explicitly stated for today, these projects can be unpredictable), it's a mess. You're better off running laps in your living room.

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Immediate Danger: Low Visibility and Unstable Footing.** The special weather statement for heavy flurries and blowing snow means conditions can change rapidly. Reduced visibility combined with accumulating, slippery snow creates a high risk for falls and can make it hard for motorists to see you. Don't underestimate this.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Extreme Cold Factor:** While the reported temperature is around -3.8°C (or -4°C, 25°F according to Environment Canada), those 50 km/h wind gusts will make it *feel* a whole lot colder. Exposed skin will get frostbitten fast. Layer up like you're an onion, and then add another layer. Seriously.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
**Plan Your Route (or don't):** If you absolutely *must* get outside, stick to well-plowed main roads where visibility is better and surfaces are somewhat clearer. Assume anything off the beaten path, especially the less-maintained sections of the Trans Canada Trail or trails in more wooded areas like Ardagh Bluffs, will be rough, deep, and potentially dangerous. Tell someone where you're going and when you expect to be back. And for the love of all that is holy, wear reflective gear.
</AlertBox>
