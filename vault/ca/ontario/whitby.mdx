## Why today is/isn't the day:

<BulletList>
  <li>**Wind Chill is a Real Kick in the Teeth:** The actual temperature is hovering around -3°C, but with the wind blowing off Lake Ontario, it's going to *feel* more like a brutal -18°C in our neck of the woods, especially in the morning. That's not just "brisk"; that's "risk of frostbite in minutes" territory if you're not properly covered. Your joints are going to feel every single degree of that.</li>
  <li>**Ozone is Hanging Around:** We've got a moderate Air Quality Index (AQI) of 71 today, with ozone being the main culprit. For us regular folks, it might just feel a bit thick, but if you've got any respiratory issues – asthma, COPD, even just a sensitive set of lungs – this isn't going to be your best breathing day. Best to keep those workouts low-intensity or inside if you're susceptible.</li>
  <li>**Trail Troubles Continue:** If you were hoping for a serene long run along the water, think again. Sections of the Waterfront Trail are still a mess. Specifically, the bit through Victoria Fields Park by Whitby Harbour is closed for dredging until October 2026, and you'll hit a detour there. Also, good luck trying to get through Lynde Shores Conservation Area, as wetland restoration work has that part of the Waterfront Trail temporarily shut down for the next four years, starting back in November 2025. So, if you manage to avoid frostbite and ozone, you might just run into a construction barrier. Fun.</li>
</BulletList>

## Workout Safely, If You Insist

<AlertBox type="danger">
  **Extreme Cold Warning:** That -18°C wind chill is no joke. Exposed skin can get frostbitten quickly. Dress in layers, cover all skin (hat, gloves, face mask are essential), and seriously reconsider prolonged outdoor activity, especially in the morning. Stay hydrated, even if you don't feel thirsty, and know the signs of hypothermia.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
  **Moderate Air Quality for Sensitive Lungs:** With an AQI of 71 and ozone as the dominant pollutant, people with asthma, heart conditions, or other respiratory sensitivities should significantly reduce or reschedule strenuous outdoor activity. It's not "stay indoors at all costs" levels, but don't push it. Pay attention to how your lungs feel.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="info">
  **Know Your Detours:** If you're hitting the Waterfront Trail, plan ahead. The closure around Victoria Fields Park means a detour is in effect. Similarly, avoid the Lynde Shores Conservation Area section of the trail entirely for now. Don't be that person ignoring signs and getting stuck in mud or a construction zone. Your Instagram post won't be worth it.
</AlertBox>
