## Whitby Run Down: February 28, 2026

You're looking at a day that *could* be worse, but definitely isn't ideal for smashing any personal bests without some serious awareness. While the skies are technically clear, the temperature of 3.2°C is deceptive. Environment Canada and other forecasts are showing wind chills making it feel more like -10°C to -12°C this afternoon, so layer up, or you'll regret it.

### Trail Trouble & Pavement Pains

Let's cut right to it: if you were planning a scenic long run along the **Waterfront Trail**, especially east of Whitby Harbour, you might want to rethink your route. Reports from as recently as February 19th indicate significant, ungroomed snow cover. "Far too difficult to ride or walk through" is the phrase, and I concur. You're risking a twisted ankle trying to navigate that deep, uneven snow.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Waterfront Trail Closure/Detour:** A section of the Waterfront Trail through Victoria Fields Park, near Whitby Harbour, remains closed until October 2026 due to dredging. A temporary asphalt trail detour is in place, but be mindful of ongoing work in the area.
</AlertBox>

And it's not just the trails. Our roads are a perpetual construction zone, as per usual. The multi-year Columbus Road widening project means ongoing closures between Country Lane and Cedarbrook Trail, with major work ramping up in 2026. Detour signs are up, so pay attention. You've also got the White Bridge construction continuing through winter, and minor repairs on various culverts and bridges across town through June 2026. If your route involves Simcoe Street or Conlin Road, be aware that Durham Region Transit has rerouted some lines due to construction, meaning more road changes and potential traffic.

Your lungs get a moderate workout challenge today, not just from your efforts. The Air Quality Index is currently at 75, which is considered moderate, with ozone as the dominant pollutant. This isn't severe, but if you're sensitive or planning a high-intensity session, you might notice it more.

Pollen levels are mercifully low, which is good news for anyone typically suffering from seasonal allergies, even if there's not much blooming in late February anyway.

**Dress for the *Feel*, Not Just the Temp:** With that wind chill, a light jacket over a long-sleeve might not cut it. Think layers, gloves, and maybe even a hat to protect against that biting cold, especially early morning or late evening.

<AlertBox type="danger">

Overall, today's a day for sticking to cleared, familiar routes if you're heading out. Be smart, stay safe, and maybe just hit the treadmill for once. Your joints will thank you.
