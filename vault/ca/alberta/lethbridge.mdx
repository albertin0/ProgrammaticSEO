We're talking gusts that could hit 110 km/h. These aren't just "a bit breezy" conditions; these are "hold onto your hat, your dog, and possibly your car door" winds. They're expected to pick up early this morning, peak right through the afternoon, and only start calming down by early Friday.

### Extreme Wind Warning!
Avoid outdoor activities today. Wind gusts up to 110 km/h are expected in Lethbridge. This poses a significant risk for falls, flying debris, and even difficulty maintaining control, especially on exposed areas like the River Valley Trails or near Whoop-Up Drive. Secure anything loose in your yard, and reconsider any plans to head out for a run or ride. Driving conditions are also expected to be difficult.

I know, I know, the temperature is a mild 8°C and the sky's clear, which usually screams "get outside!" But that wind will make it feel a lot colder and significantly less enjoyable. Your lungs will be working overtime just to pull in air against that kind of resistance, and your joints will be under unnecessary strain trying to stabilize you. Think about falling branches, or even getting knocked off balance. It's just not worth it.

On the bright side, pollen is practically non-existent right now, so at least your allergies won't be raging if you *do* brave the elements (which you shouldn't).
