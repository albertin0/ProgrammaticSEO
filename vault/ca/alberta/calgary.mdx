### Wind & Weather Woes

Today's the kind of day that looks decent on paper, but the reality on the ground (or in your face) is going to be significantly less pleasant. We're currently sitting at a balmy 5°C, which, let's be honest, feels like spring after the deep freeze we sometimes get. But that warmth comes with a serious catch: intense winds. Expect sustained speeds to pick up, gusting potentially to 70 km/h. As a PT, I can tell you that kind of sustained headwind or crosswind isn't just annoying; it can mess with your gait, strain your knees and hips as you fight for stability, and make cycling outright dangerous.

And don't get too comfortable with the clear skies. The real show starts tonight. Environment Canada is predicting a significant winter storm. Calgary itself is looking at 5 to 10 centimetres of snow starting late this evening and continuing through Friday. If you're planning an evening run, be aware that conditions will deteriorate rapidly. Visibility will be compromised, and those pleasant temps will plummet, leading to wind chills that'll make you question your life choices.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**High Winds & Impending Snow:** Strong winds (gusts up to 70 km/h) are expected today, making outdoor activities challenging and potentially hazardous. Visibility will drop significantly tonight as 5-10 cm of snow starts to fall. Plan accordingly, or better yet, stay inside.
</AlertBox>

### Pathway Pain: The Saga Continues

If you're a regular on the Bow River Pathway, you already know the drill: closures, detours, and more closures. Many of these aren't new, but they're *still* there and impacting your routes.

Sections of the Bow River Pathway around Parkdale Boulevard N.W., Montgomery Boulevard N.W., and the North Edworthy Parking Lot are closed for watermain repairs, some until Spring or even December 2026. This isn't just a minor inconvenience; these are significant stretches that force you onto less-than-ideal detours.

And speaking of closures, the Douglas Fir Trail, a beloved spot for many, continues to be a mess. A section has been closed since November 2023 due to erosion. While they tried to open a western portion last November, a recent inspection on February 18, 2026, found unsafe conditions, and it's closed *again*. Repair work on a smaller section above the Bow River Regional Pathway is ongoing, with substantial completion expected by October 2026, but a full reopening of the broader trail isn't even funded yet, potentially pushing a full return out to the 2027-2030 budget cycle. So much for quick fixes, eh?

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Long-Term Trail Closures Persist:** Major sections of the Bow River Pathway, particularly in the NW around Parkdale, Montgomery, and Edworthy Park, remain closed for long-term watermain repairs and slope remediation.
