## The Cold, Hard Truth

Look, I love our city's outdoor spaces as much as the next runner, but today is not the day for heroics. We’re deep into February, and Mother Nature apparently decided we needed one last brutal slap before thinking about spring. That -20.5°C is just the start; Factor in any breeze, and exposed skin starts paying the price in minutes. Frostbite doesn't care about your Strava segments.

<AlertBox type="danger">
### Extreme Cold Warning: Stay Indoors!
With actual temperatures at -20.5°C and wind chills potentially dipping to -40°C, exposed skin can freeze *fast*. This isn't just uncomfortable; it's dangerous. Your lungs will also take a beating trying to warm up that frigid air. If you *must* go out, cover every inch of skin, layer aggressively, and keep it incredibly short. Seriously, re-think it.
</AlertBox>

And let's talk about the ground beneath your feet. We've had enough snow this month to ensure that any moisture on trails and sidewalks has now turned into a solid, unforgiving layer of ice. Trying to hit your stride on the Groat Road pathway or around the Legislature grounds? You're setting yourself up for a potential joint injury. Slips, trips, and falls are a huge risk when conditions are this slick. Even if it looks clear, assume black ice is lurking.

## Lungs & Air Quality

While the pollen count is blessedly low (because nothing can grow in this tundra), the air quality isn't doing us any favours either. An AQI of 77 with inhalable particulate matter means your lungs are working harder than usual. Combine that with the searing cold, and you've got a recipe for a truly miserable and potentially harmful run. Save the heavy breathing for indoors today.

<AlertBox type="warning">
### Moderate Air Quality - Particulate Matter
An AQI of 77, with fine particulate matter (&lt;10µm) being the dominant pollutant, means sensitive groups should be cautious. Even if you're not sensitive, adding cold-weather breathing to this equation isn't ideal for lung health. Consider shorter workouts or staying inside.
</AlertBox>
