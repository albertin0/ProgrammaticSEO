## The Saskatoon Deep Freeze isn't a "Challenge," It's a Hazard

Seriously, who looks at -22°C and thinks, "Perfect day for a run"? Your body works overtime just to keep your core warm, let alone power a workout. Breathing in air that cold, especially with moderate pollutant levels, is a recipe for bronchial irritation and muscle strain. Every lungful is a sharp, icy knife.

We had a "SNOW EVENT" just a couple of days ago, and while the skies are clear now, that doesn't magically melt away the packed snow and ice. ### Extreme Cold: Frostbite is a Real Threat
With temperatures feeling like -29°C, exposed skin can get frostbite in minutes. We're well into the territory where Saskatoon activates its Level II cold weather plan for dangerous conditions. Your fingers, toes, nose, and ears are all highly susceptible. This isn't just discomfort; it's tissue damage.

### Mind the Ice, Everywhere
Recent snowfall and ongoing freezing temperatures mean sidewalks, trails, and quieter residential streets are likely covered in slick ice. A simple fall can mean a sprain, a fracture, or a head injury. Keep your eyes peeled and assume every patch of pavement is a hidden hazard.
