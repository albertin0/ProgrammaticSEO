The weather app says "Clear" and 8.5°C, which sounds idyllic until you factor in everything else. Low pollen is a small win, I guess, for those of us not allergic to our own city's constant state of flux.

### Your Lungs: Ozone and Other Annoyances

That AQI of 79 is squarely in the "Moderate" zone, primarily thanks to Ozone. For most people, you'll be fine, but if you have asthma or other respiratory issues, you might notice some irritation. Don't be a hero; dial back the intensity if you're huffing and puffing more than usual. This isn't the day to push your VO2 max to its absolute limit.

### Your Joints: Mind the Wind, Mind the Gaps

The breeze, clocking in with gusts around 35 km/h, is enough to make you work a little harder and potentially throw off your stride. Be extra cautious on exposed bridges or sections of the **Stanley Park Seawall** where you don't have tree cover to block the wind. Stability is key, especially if you're coming back from an ankle tweak or a grumpy knee.

<AlertBox type="danger">
#### Hastings Creek Trail is Permanently Closed
Listen up, North Shore runners and hikers: the central section of **Hastings Creek Trail** is PERMANENTLY CLOSED. After two decades of trying to fight erosion and slope failures, the District of North Vancouver has thrown in the towel. The southern section is also closed due to a bridge failure. This isn't a temporary detour; find a new route. Seriously, don't risk it for a photo op.
</AlertBox>

<AlertBox type="warning">
#### Deas Island Park and Delta Nature Reserve: Access Denied
Over at **Deas Island Regional Park**, don't expect to loop around like usual. Sections of Riverside Walk, Dyke Loop Trail, and Island Tip Trail are closed, and the Sand Dune Trail and western tip of the island are fully shut down until 2030 thanks to the Fraser River Tunnel Project. Meanwhile, the access road to the **Delta Nature Reserve** is also closed due to flooding and washouts. Check Metro Vancouver's park alerts before you head out, unless you enjoy running into literal roadblocks.
</AlertBox>

### Construction Everywhere, Always

It's Vancouver, so of course there's construction. The Broadway Subway Extension is still chewing up the city, creating major disruptions. Areas like the River District are under heavy development, which means more trucks, more noise, and more unexpected detours. Keep your head on a swivel.

<AlertBox type="info">
#### Just a heads up on future events
While nothing major is blocking your path *today*, remember that the FIFA World Cup 2026 is going to mess with areas around BC Place and False Creek throughout the year. Plan ahead if you're training near there later on.
</AlertBox>

So, yeah, you *can* work out outside. Just go in with your eyes open, your lungs moderately irritated, and a backup plan for when your favourite trail is unexpectedly a construction site or, you know, just gone.
