## Your Knees, Lungs, and Sanity in Victoria Today

So, it's clear out, a brisk 5.2 degrees Celsius. Sounds decent for a run along Dallas Road, right? Maybe, if you don't mind the moderate Air Quality Index of 67, with ozone as the dominant pollutant. It’s not terrible, but if you've got sensitive lungs, you'll feel it more than usual, especially on those longer efforts. Low pollen means no snot rockets for most of us, so that's a small win, I guess.

The real headache, as usual, is our beloved, yet perpetually disrupted, trail system. You want to hit the Galloping Goose? Good luck with that. The section between Viaduct Park and Cecelia Road is still shut down due to "slope instability," and they're talking about "temporary mitigation measures" while we all wait for an engineering report. It's been like this for ages, so plan your detour around it, or just accept you're adding an extra half-mile of urban navigation to your route.

<AlertBox type="warning">
**Major Galloping Goose Trail Closures Ahead:** Starting Monday, March 2nd (so, just a few days from now), a six-week closure kicks off for the Galloping Goose *east of Atkins Road in View Royal* to replace a trail bridge over Six Mile Road. They'll have detours and traffic control, but expect delays and confusion. Also, if you were planning on the E&N Trail in Esquimalt, part of it between Admirals Road and Stancombe Place is closed Monday from 9 AM to 3 PM for borehole testing. You just can't win with these trails.
</AlertBox>

And let's not forget the grand plan: the CRD Regional Trestles Renewal, Trails Widening and Lighting Project. This multi-year undertaking means ongoing pre-construction work and eventual closures on both the Galloping Goose (specifically between Selkirk Trestle and Grange Road) and the Lochside Regional Trail (between the Switch Bridge and McKenzie Avenue/Borden Street). Basically, consider all your usual routes subject to random detours and construction zones for the foreseeable future. Don't get too attached to your Strava segments.

<AlertBox type="danger">
**Watch Out for Drivers!** VicPD reported back in January that a pedestrian or cyclist was struck *every single day* in Victoria so far this year. That's not a typo. Every. Single. Day. This isn't just a hazard; it's a terrifying statistic. Assume every driver is looking at their phone, chugging coffee, or just generally oblivious. Make eye contact, wear bright colours, and for the love of all that is holy, stick to the detours and crosswalks. Your life isn't worth saving two minutes by jaywalking.
</AlertBox>

So, today's not exactly prime conditions, but if you bundle up, avoid the trail construction, and stay hyper-vigilant on the roads, you can probably get your miles in. Just manage your expectations and don't assume your usual path will be clear.
